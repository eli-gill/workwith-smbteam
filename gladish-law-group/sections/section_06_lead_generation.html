Lead Generation

Gladish Law Group has earned an exceptional reputation in Northwest Indiana over three decades of complex personal injury and civil rights work — but that reputation is not currently being amplified by any paid digital marketing. While Lerner & Rowe, Stracci Law Group, and Hilbrich Law Firm invest aggressively in Google Ads, Local Service Ads, and social media, this firm's digital marketing consists primarily of its website and organic SEO — a significant and growing gap in a high-competition Chicago suburb market.

The Problem
  • Contact form is located on a separate page (dgladishlaw.com/contact-page/) and is not visible above the fold on the homepage — visitors who are ready to act cannot submit their information immediately
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile and desktop scores are pending manual verification at pagespeed.web.dev — this must be completed before final report delivery. Navigate to pagespeed.web.dev and run https://dgladishlaw.com/ to record exact scores.
  • No live chat present on the website — after-hours visitors and weekend browsers have no real-time way to connect with the firm
  • Video content not confirmed on the homepage — for a firm with David Gladish's credentials and $82 million in results, an attorney introduction video is a powerful trust-builder that is currently absent
Blind Spot: Competitors may be bidding on "Gladish Law Group" and "David Gladish" as Google Ads keywords right now — capturing people who already know the firm and search for it by name, then routing them to competing law firms instead. This is one of the most common and costly vulnerabilities for high-reputation firms that are not running their own ads.
The Positive
  • Active blog with 28+ pages of content — the most recent post was September 28, 2025 — demonstrating a sustained content investment that supports organic SEO
  • Multiple geo-targeted landing pages built for Highland, Crown Point, and surrounding Lake County communities — a meaningful SEO investment that most small firms have not made
The Gap
  • Without a contact form visible above the fold, a meaningful percentage of motivated visitors leave before submitting their information — that is leads the firm already paid for in reputation and SEO that never convert
  • Without live chat, after-hours visitors who are ready to speak to an attorney right now go to a competitor who has 24/7 availability — and injury prospects rarely call back the next morning
  • A slow mobile experience or unoptimized site structure suppresses Google search rankings, compounding the visibility gap against competitors who have invested in technical SEO
A website that does not convert is not a marketing asset — it is a business card. Gladish Law Group's credentials deserve a site that turns visits into consultations around the clock, not just during business hours.

The Gap
  • Without fixing the Martindale NAP inconsistency and consolidating the duplicate Yelp profiles, Google's local algorithm receives conflicting signals about this business — suppressing local pack rankings
  • Without a structured review request process, the firm will continue to fall further behind competitors in the primary local ranking signal; the review count gap is a race that gets harder to close the longer it is ignored
Lerner and Rowe Injury Attorneys — operating in the Lake County market with 4,326 Google reviews versus Gladish Law Group's 266. Their review volume alone gives Google a clear dominance signal — and it translates directly into 3-pack placement for every high-intent personal injury search in the region.
Ranking consistently in the local 3-pack for personal injury searches in Highland and Crown Point means the phone rings with qualified leads without David having to personally earn each referral — that is the difference between a practice and a business.
