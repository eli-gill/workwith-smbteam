Fix NAP Inconsistency on Martindale.com
Lead Gen Quick Win
Why It Matters
Martindale.com lists a different phone number (219-339-4176) than the firm's primary line (219-838-1900). Google's local ranking algorithm compares name, address, and phone number (NAP) across directories — inconsistencies are treated as conflicting signals and directly suppress local pack rankings.
The Opportunity
Correcting this single listing takes less than an hour and sends a clean, consistent signal to Google — improving local SEO authority across all three practice areas without spending a dollar on ads.

Move Contact Form Above the Fold on the Homepage
Intake Quick Win
Why It Matters
The contact form is currently on a separate page at dgladishlaw.com/contact-page/ — invisible to visitors who land on the homepage and do not navigate further. Research consistently shows that contact forms placed above the fold convert at dramatically higher rates than those buried on separate pages.
The Opportunity
Embedding a short contact form directly on the homepage — "Tell us about your case" with name, phone, and case type — converts passive browsers into active leads without requiring them to make the first phone call. This is one of the highest-ROI changes available for zero additional ad spend.
Implement a Systematic Post-Case Review Request Process
Lead Gen Long-Term Play
Why It Matters
Google's local ranking algorithm treats review volume as a primary signal. Gladish Law Group has 266 Google reviews — strong quality but well below the review counts of competitors occupying top local pack positions. The gap widens every month without a structured process to ask satisfied clients for a review.
Who Is Doing It Better
Lerner and Rowe Injury Attorneys — 4,326 Google reviews versus Gladish Law Group's 266; their volume advantage signals to Google that they are the dominant firm in the market, which directly translates into higher local pack placement on every search.
The Opportunity
A simple post-settlement review request — sent via text or email at case close — could add dozens of reviews per year from clients who are already happy and simply were never asked. Closing this gap improves local pack rankings for all three of the firm's practice areas simultaneously.
Hire a Dedicated Intake Coordinator
Team Long-Term Play
Why It Matters
Without a dedicated intake coordinator, every new lead that comes in competes directly with attorney billing time. As marketing spend increases, this bottleneck gets worse — more calls, same number of people to answer them. A trained intake person is the single hire that makes every other investment in lead generation pay off.
The Opportunity
A dedicated intake coordinator handles every initial inquiry, screens for case quality, books consultations, and follows up with unsigned leads — freeing David Gladish and the associate attorneys to focus entirely on case work. One signed case per month pays for this hire several times over.
Implement Case Attribution and Cost-Per-Case Tracking
Profit Quick Win
Why It Matters
Without tracking which marketing channels produce which cases — and what each case costs to acquire and litigate — the firm cannot know whether its marketing investment is working. This means growing the ad budget is a guess rather than a managed decision, and profitable case types get the same attention as unprofitable ones.
The Opportunity
Setting up a simple source-tracking system (asking every new client "how did you find us?" and recording it) combined with basic case expense tracking by type gives the firm the data to make every future marketing decision with confidence — and to know exactly which cases are worth taking.
