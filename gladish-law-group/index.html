Here Is Where Gladish Law Group Stands Today

Gladish Law Group is a three-attorney personal injury and civil trial firm headquartered in Highland, Indiana — with a second office in Crown Point — that has built an exceptional courtroom reputation over three decades, securing more than $82 million in verdicts and settlements for clients across Northwest Indiana and Illinois. Founder David Gladish holds the highest credentials in plaintiff personal injury law: Board Certified Civil Trial Specialist, Indiana SuperLawyers member every year since 2010, Best Lawyers in America, and a lifetime Million Dollar Advocate. The single most important finding from this audit: the firm's reputation and results are outstanding — but they are not being amplified by any paid digital marketing, while more aggressive competitors are actively investing in every channel where injured clients are searching right now.

Stage 6 is where Gladish Law Group gets to stop winning cases through David's personal reputation and starts winning them through a system that runs on its own — so David can focus on the trial work he does best, or step back and let the firm carry on without him.

Where You Need To Be

  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where Gladish Law Group is and where it deserves to be is not a talent gap or a reputation gap — it is a marketing and systems gap, and that is entirely fixable.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

For a personal injury firm where each signed case represents thousands of dollars in contingency fees, a slow or inconsistent intake process is one of the most expensive problems a law firm can have — and it is almost always invisible to the owner who is focused on winning cases. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No live chat on the website — prospects who visit after hours or on weekends cannot reach anyone in real time; injury clients are ready to act in the moment, and that moment passes quickly
  • Contact form is on a separate page — not visible on the homepage; visitors who are motivated to reach out face an extra step that reduces conversion significantly
  • No confirmed dedicated intake team or defined intake process — for a three-attorney firm, incoming leads likely compete directly with attorney billing time, creating a bottleneck at the front door
  • No confirmed follow-up sequence for leads who submit a form or call and do not sign immediately — the majority of prospects need more than one touchpoint before committing, and without a follow-up system, those leads go cold
Blind Spot: Most injury prospects visit multiple law firm websites before calling anyone. If Gladish Law Group's site does not have a fast, frictionless path to connect — and a competitor's site does — the prospect calls the competitor first. They rarely call back after that.
The Positive
  • Free consultation is clearly offered and the primary phone number (219-838-1900) is featured throughout the site — the intent to be accessible is there, even if the conversion infrastructure is not yet built out
  • Full attorney bios with detailed credentials give prospects the confidence to reach out — when they do find the contact path, the trust-building elements are in place
The Gap
  • For a firm where a single signed client can generate $10,000–$50,000 in contingency fees, a prospect who submits a form after hours and does not hear back until the next morning is a lost case — and often one that calls a competitor before 9am
  • Without a dedicated intake team, launching a paid marketing campaign would send more calls to attorneys who are already billing time — creating a new bottleneck that prevents the marketing investment from paying off
  • Without a follow-up sequence for unsigned leads, the firm's revenue is limited to people who say yes immediately — that is a fraction of the total opportunity sitting in the firm's existing inquiry volume
Building a defined intake process means David Gladish spends his time in courtrooms and on case strategy — not fielding initial calls and screening prospects who called at 9pm on a Saturday.

Profit Plan Assessment

Gladish Law Group operates entirely on contingency — which means the firm's revenue is real but unpredictable, tied to case outcomes, settlement timelines, and the mix of case types being signed at any given time. Without a profit plan that tracks acquisition cost per case type and margin by practice area, scaling the caseload may mean scaling the workload without a proportional increase in what the firm actually keeps. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • No confirmed tracking of marketing or acquisition cost per case — without this, it is impossible to know which practice areas or lead sources are producing the most profitable results
  • Contingency fee revenue is inherently unpredictable without systematic case pipeline tracking; cash flow planning for a firm with complex litigation timelines is extremely difficult without it
  • Investing in paid advertising without a cost-per-case attribution system would make it impossible to evaluate campaign ROI — resulting in ad spend decisions being made on gut feeling rather than data
  • No confirmed separation of case expense tracking — without clear financial controls, litigation costs on complex med mal and civil rights cases can erode net fees significantly
Blind Spot: Medical malpractice cases are often the highest-gross-fee cases in this firm's portfolio, but they are also among the most expensive to litigate — with expert witness fees, medical record costs, and extended timelines. Without tracking cost per case type, the firm could be generating strong gross fees on med mal while netting less per hour than on straightforward auto accident cases. The answer may surprise you.
The Positive
  • $82 million in verdicts and settlements since 2006 demonstrates the firm consistently wins high-value cases — the raw revenue potential is proven and real, giving a strong foundation to build profit discipline around
  • Three distinct practice area tracks — personal injury, medical malpractice, and civil rights — give the firm the ability to manage caseload mix and spread financial risk across case types and timelines
The Gap
  • Without marketing spend tracking and attribution, it is impossible to know which ad channels are generating the most profitable cases — meaning ad budget decisions will always be guesses, and the wrong guesses will be made repeatedly
  • Without cash flow forecasting tied to the case pipeline, the firm cannot reliably plan team hires, capital investments, or personal owner distributions — putting David at the mercy of timing he cannot control
  • The difference between a good year and a great year for a contingency firm is not just more cases — it is the right mix of cases, tracked for margin, with expenses controlled; that is how revenue becomes real wealth
A profit plan tied to case mix, marketing attribution, and expense tracking transforms a firm that earns well into a firm that keeps well — and that is the difference between working hard and building real, lasting wealth.
