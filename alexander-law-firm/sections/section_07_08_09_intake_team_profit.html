Intake Assessment

Adam is the intake system at The Alexander Law Firm — every inquiry, every case evaluation, and every new client conversation flows through him personally, with no defined process, no backup, and no coverage when he is unavailable. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Adam personally handles all intake — there is no intake process that functions when he is in a hearing, on vacation, or otherwise unavailable
  • All intake is manual with no CRM, no automated follow-up, and no structured evaluation sequence for new inquiries — leads that do not sign on first contact have no follow-up path
  • Current intake service costs $1,500 per month but operates without AI-backed automation, consistent qualification scripts, or after-hours coverage
  • No after-hours coverage for inquiries that arrive evenings or weekends — potential clients searching for urgent help in crisis moments may reach a competitor before Adam is available
Blind Spot: Every client Adam has ever lost was most likely lost in the first 24 hours. Research consistently shows that the first attorney to respond wins the case — and with an entirely manual intake system, Adam will always be at a structural disadvantage after hours, on weekends, and during hearings. The revenue being lost is invisible because there is no system tracking it.
The Gap
  • At a $5,000+ average case value, a single missed or unreturned inquiry per week represents over $20,000 per month in potential revenue that never materializes — and Adam has no way to know how often this is happening
  • Without a documented intake process, Adam cannot delegate intake even when he is ready to hire — there is nothing to train someone on, which makes the first hire slower and less effective
  • Manual intake is the primary structural reason the firm cannot scale: every new case inquiry creates more personal time demand on Adam, making growth a burden rather than a benefit
Building an intake system that works without Adam is not just an efficiency upgrade — it is the first concrete step toward the personal freedom he described wanting on this call.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form confirmed only on the /contact-us page — not observed above the fold on the homepage; a visitor who does not actively navigate to the contact page has no visible path to reach the firm
  • No live chat widget confirmed on the site — after-hours website visitors, who represent a significant share of consumer protection inquiries, have no immediate response pathway and will leave without taking action
  • For lemon law and FDCPA clients who are frustrated and searching for fast answers, a slow or friction-heavy contact experience is a disqualifier — they will call the firm that makes it easiest to reach a real person

Team Assessment

The Alexander Law Firm has no team — Adam is the firm, and every operational function from answering client calls to managing case files runs through him alone, sustained entirely by his personal time and energy. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"The firm's success is stressful, requiring constant mental engagement and limiting personal freedom, even during vacations."
The Problem
  • Adam has no employees and no plans to hire — the firm runs entirely on his personal time, which creates a hard ceiling on revenue, client capacity, and personal freedom simultaneously
  • Fear of hiring has previously prevented growth: Adam has hesitated to bring on staff and to partner with SMB Team due to the perceived burden of managing people
  • All systems are manual across every function — there is nothing documented, no process another person could follow, and no infrastructure for delegation even if a hire were made today
  • The firm is on pace for $900,000 in revenue, but none of that growth is building independence — more revenue at this stage means more of Adam's personal time, not less
Blind Spot: The belief that "I need to get more organized before I hire" is the single most common reason solo attorneys never hire. The systems do not come first — they develop in response to a hire. Building the confidence and the roadmap to make that first hire — and coaching through the management challenges that follow — is exactly what accelerates the transition from practitioner to firm owner.
The Gap
  • A solo practice with no documented systems cannot be sold at meaningful value — buyers acquire firms with processes, staff, and transferable operations, not individual attorneys whose departure would eliminate the business
  • Without a team, Adam cannot take a real vacation, accept a speaking engagement, or be unavailable for any reason without the firm suffering — personal freedom is structurally impossible in the current model
  • Reaching $900,000 in revenue without a team will create more stress, more hours, and more personal burnout — not the freedom Adam described as his goal
The firm Adam described — one that runs without him, generates consistent income, and has real sellable value — begins with one well-planned hire, done right, with the right coaching behind it.

Profit Plan Assessment

The Alexander Law Firm is generating strong revenue — over $600,000 last year and tracking toward $900,000 — but with no financial systems in place, there is no visibility into margins, no structured plan for what Adam personally keeps, and no framework for turning revenue growth into predictable personal wealth. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • All financial processes are manual — taxes, time tracking, and billing are handled without structured software, creating ongoing risk of missed revenue, compliance gaps, and financial blind spots
  • No visibility into profit margin by case type — with multiple practice areas at different complexity levels, Adam cannot determine which cases generate the highest return on his personal time
  • As revenue scales toward $900,000, manual financial management creates increasing exposure to under-reporting, unbilled work, and tax inefficiency
  • No dedicated bookkeeping relationship or fractional CFO support — the owner is managing firm finances alongside an active caseload, which means neither gets the attention it requires
Blind Spot: In a contingency-based practice where defendants pay attorney fees, the actual profitability per case type varies significantly by settlement complexity and time invested. A lemon law case that settles in 60 days may be far more profitable per hour than an FDCPA case requiring extended litigation. Without tracking this, Adam may be devoting his most valuable hours to his least profitable work — and the only way to know is to measure it.
The Positive
  • Revenue trajectory is strong and consistent — growth from approximately $500,000 to over $600,000 to a $900,000 target demonstrates real market demand and Adam's proven ability to win and close cases
The Gap
  • Without financial systems, Adam cannot make informed decisions about pricing, case selection, or the timing of his first hire — all three require knowing the numbers
  • At $600,000+ in revenue, the firm may already qualify for tax strategies that are not being leveraged — manual financial management has a direct dollar cost that compounds annually
  • No financial clarity means Adam cannot set a concrete personal income target, which makes "more profit" a feeling rather than a plan with a defined finish line
The financial systems Adam builds now will be among the first things a buyer evaluates — clean books, documented margins, and predictable revenue are what transform a profitable practice into a valuable, sellable asset.
