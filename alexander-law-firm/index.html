Here Is Where The Alexander Law Firm Stands Today

Adam Alexander has spent nearly 30 years fighting for Michigan consumers as a solo practitioner at Alexander, PLLC — handling lemon law, fair debt collection (FDCPA), and fair credit reporting (FCRA) cases out of a Southfield office where every function of the firm runs through him personally. The firm has grown to over $600,000 in annual revenue and is tracking toward $900,000 this year — but that growth has come at a cost: an all-manual, owner-dependent operation with no team, no systems, and an attorney who cannot step away without the firm feeling it.

Stage 6 is where the firm finally runs without you — generating consistent revenue, serving clients well, and building real sellable value whether you show up or not. That is exactly where this audit is designed to take you.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • All firm functions — intake, casework, billing, and client communication — flow through Adam personally with no delegation in place
  • No paid advertising has ever been run — new clients come through referrals and organic search only, with no documented lead generation system
  • All operational systems are manual: time tracking, taxes, billing, and client communications
  • The firm cannot operate independently — Adam cannot take a real vacation without the business stalling

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where you are and where you want to be is not about working harder — it is about building the systems and the team that let the firm grow without requiring you to be present for every part of it.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The site's blog has been dormant since approximately 2017 — nearly a decade without new content signals to Google that the site is not being actively maintained, which directly suppresses search rankings
  • PageSpeed scores: Could not be retrieved — myfaircreditsite.com actively blocks automated crawlers including Google's PageSpeed API; manual testing at pagespeed.web.dev is required to confirm actual scores
  • No CTA confirmed above the fold on the homepage — whether a first-time visitor takes action immediately after landing is unclear and likely inconsistent
  • Contact form confirmed only on a separate /contact-us page — a visitor who does not actively navigate there has no path to reach the firm from the homepage
Blind Spot: The firm's domain — myfaircreditsite.com — contains no firm name and no geographic signal. Visitors arriving from search may not immediately recognize this as "The Alexander Law Firm" in Southfield, and the domain forfeits local SEO authority that a branded, location-specific domain would carry. Competitors with domains like "michiganlemlaw.com" or their firm name in the URL have a structural SEO advantage on this point alone.
The Positive
  • Michigan geo-targeting landing pages exist for Southfield and Detroit across multiple practice areas — a meaningful SEO foundation that most solo practitioners never build
  • Attorney bio page, practice area service pages, a resources section, and a reviews page are all present — the content structure is in place and ready to be optimized
The Gap
  • A visitor who cannot find a phone number or contact form without navigating away from the homepage will leave — and at a $5,000+ average case value, that exit costs real money
  • A dormant blog with no posts since 2017 actively reduces the site's content freshness score, pushing it down in organic rankings over time while competitors with active content move up
  • Without confirmed PageSpeed scores, the site may be failing Core Web Vitals — a direct Google ranking factor that penalizes slow or unoptimized mobile pages across all search results
A website that is fast, conversion-optimized, and built to turn visitors into calls is the foundation for a firm that brings in clients without Adam having to personally chase each one.

Profit Plan Assessment

The Alexander Law Firm is generating strong revenue — over $600,000 last year and tracking toward $900,000 — but with no financial systems in place, there is no visibility into margins, no structured plan for what Adam personally keeps, and no framework for turning revenue growth into predictable personal wealth. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • All financial processes are manual — taxes, time tracking, and billing are handled without structured software, creating ongoing risk of missed revenue, compliance gaps, and financial blind spots
  • No visibility into profit margin by case type — with multiple practice areas at different complexity levels, Adam cannot determine which cases generate the highest return on his personal time
  • As revenue scales toward $900,000, manual financial management creates increasing exposure to under-reporting, unbilled work, and tax inefficiency
  • No dedicated bookkeeping relationship or fractional CFO support — the owner is managing firm finances alongside an active caseload, which means neither gets the attention it requires
Blind Spot: In a contingency-based practice where defendants pay attorney fees, the actual profitability per case type varies significantly by settlement complexity and time invested. A lemon law case that settles in 60 days may be far more profitable per hour than an FDCPA case requiring extended litigation. Without tracking this, Adam may be devoting his most valuable hours to his least profitable work — and the only way to know is to measure it.
The Positive
  • Revenue trajectory is strong and consistent — growth from approximately $500,000 to over $600,000 to a $900,000 target demonstrates real market demand and Adam's proven ability to win and close cases
The Gap
  • Without financial systems, Adam cannot make informed decisions about pricing, case selection, or the timing of his first hire — all three require knowing the numbers
  • At $600,000+ in revenue, the firm may already qualify for tax strategies that are not being leveraged — manual financial management has a direct dollar cost that compounds annually
  • No financial clarity means Adam cannot set a concrete personal income target, which makes "more profit" a feeling rather than a plan with a defined finish line
The financial systems Adam builds now will be among the first things a buyer evaluates — clean books, documented margins, and predictable revenue are what transform a profitable practice into a valuable, sellable asset.
