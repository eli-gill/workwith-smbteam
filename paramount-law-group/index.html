Here Is Where Paramount Law Group Stands Today

Donna Gee has built Paramount Law Group into a ten-person Calgary practice over the past decade, handling estates and probate, guardianship and trusteeship, corporate and commercial matters, and select family law files. The firm bills over $1.2 million per year and has earned strong client loyalty through referrals and Donna's community presence — but it runs almost entirely on her personal capacity, with no paid marketing, only 13 Google reviews, and a website that was deliberately left underdeveloped. The most critical finding: Donna has the revenue, the reputation, and the ambition to build a firm she can sell — but the systems, team structure, and marketing presence needed to make that happen do not yet exist.

Stage 6 is the stage where Donna can spend two months a year in Mexico, keep a Tuesday-through-Thursday schedule, and own a firm that builds value without her — which is exactly what she has described, and exactly where we are taking her.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Growth depends almost entirely on Donna's personal referrals and speaking engagements — no marketing system running
  • HR and people management consume 8–10 hours every week, with no KPIs or accountability structure in place
  • No paid advertising has ever been run — 13 Google reviews vs. competitors with 381–467
  • Owner takes home ~$100k on $1.2M in billings — well below what this revenue level should support

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
Every stage between where Donna is today and where she wants to be comes down to the same thing: building a firm that runs when she is not there — and that starts with systems, accountability, and a predictable marketing engine, all of which we can build together starting now.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Website Audit

"Website intentionally underdeveloped to deter recruiter poaching."
The Problem
  • The site was deliberately kept minimal — which means it has no practice area landing pages, no conversion architecture, and no content that search engines can rank for targeted Calgary keywords
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved during research (site blocks automated analysis tools) — Donna should run this manually at pagespeed.web.dev; WordPress sites without optimization typically score 40–65 on mobile
  • No CTA is visible above the fold on the homepage — visitors who arrive have no immediate prompt to call, schedule, or take any action
  • Contact form is on a separate /contact/ page only — not embedded on practice area pages where intent is highest
Blind Spot: The strategy of keeping the website underdeveloped to deter recruiters is costing the firm far more in missed client inquiries than it saves in recruitment friction. Every month, people in Calgary search for "estate lawyer Calgary," "probate lawyer Calgary," and "guardianship application Alberta" and find Kahane Law or McLeod Law — not Paramount. That is not a recruiter problem. That is a revenue problem.
The Positive
  • Individual attorney bio pages exist for at least three lawyers — a foundation that can be strengthened into trust-building content
  • A public testimonials page exists at /testimonials/ — positive social proof that is not yet connected to a review-generation strategy
The Gap
  • Without practice area landing pages and local SEO content, the site cannot rank — meaning every search for the firm's target services goes to a competitor who has invested in these pages
  • A homepage with no above-fold CTA means even the visitors who do find the site are converting at a fraction of what they should — the firm is losing leads it already paid (with Donna's time) to generate
  • Without PageSpeed optimization and mobile performance, Google's algorithm ranks the site lower — compounding the visibility gap
A rebuilt, conversion-optimized website is not a vanity project — it is the foundation that makes every other marketing investment work, and the online face of a firm Donna eventually wants to sell.

Intake Assessment

Paramount Law Group has basic intake filters in place — minimum retainer requirements and some Cosmolex automation — but intake is largely manual with no defined follow-up sequence, no performance tracking, and a front desk that is acknowledged to be underperforming. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
"Front-desk underperformance" and "repeated explanations" — staffing issues at the first point of client contact are costing the firm real revenue every week.
The Problem
  • No structured intake protocol — inquiries are handled differently depending on who answers, with no defined script, qualification criteria, or urgency standard
  • No after-hours coverage — prospects who call evenings or weekends reach voicemail and often do not leave a message, calling a competitor instead before morning
  • No lead tracking or intake reporting — the firm cannot see conversion rates, average time-to-response, or how many inquiries are being lost before they ever reach a lawyer
  • Front desk performance gaps mean that the first impression for many prospective clients is inconsistent — a high-value estate or corporate prospect may hang up before they ever experience Donna's expertise
Blind Spot: Estate planning and guardianship clients are often in emotionally difficult situations — caring for an aging parent, dealing with a recent death, facing a family health crisis. They call looking for reassurance and competence from the first moment. An undertrained or inconsistent front desk is not just a conversion problem — it is a trust problem that can cost the firm its most valuable case types before a consultation is ever scheduled.
The Gap
  • At a 15% close rate and $3,500 average case value, every 10 mishandled inquiries per month costs the firm approximately $5,250 in revenue — improving intake conversion by even 5 percentage points is worth more than most paid ad campaigns
  • Without intake tracking, the firm has no way to know whether lead generation investments are working — every marketing dollar spent on ads or SEO is going into a black box
  • A firm that cannot consistently convert inbound inquiries cannot be sold at a premium — buyers want predictable systems, not owner-dependent processes
Donna's exit goal requires a firm that converts leads predictably without her involvement — that starts with intake, and intake is fixable faster than almost anything else in this audit.

Profit Plan Assessment

Paramount Law Group bills over $1.2 million per year but collects approximately $800,000 — and the owner takes home roughly $100,000, which is less than what Donna could earn as a salaried lawyer at another firm. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"2025 billed ~$1.2–1.25M; collected ~$800k; AR $150–175k (estate/farm tied); debt $100–125k; owner pay ~$100k (wants $200k)."
The Problem
  • $150–175k in accounts receivable is tied in estate and farm matters with no clear collection timeline — this is working capital the firm has already earned but cannot access
  • $100–125k in debt (line of credit and taxes) is creating cash flow pressure that makes it harder to invest in the team improvements and systems the firm needs to grow
  • Complex wills and estate files are being underquoted — scope creep on the firm's highest-value work is silently eroding margins; Donna acknowledged that complex wills are sometimes completed at a loss relative to the time invested
  • Limited KPIs and ad hoc reporting mean there is no clear visibility into which practice areas, case types, or lawyers are most profitable — decisions are made on gut feel, not financial data
Blind Spot: The $400k gap between billings ($1.2M) and collections ($800k) is not just a cash flow issue — it is a valuation issue. A buyer assessing this firm will look at collected revenue, not billed revenue. Every dollar of AR that is chronically uncollected reduces the effective multiple on exit. Cleaning up collections and tightening billing practices is one of the highest-ROI moves available before any sale conversation begins.
The Positive
  • Cosmolex is in place and actively managing trust accounting and retainer top-up automation — a financial management foundation that is ahead of many firms at this stage
  • Revenue at $1.2M+ is a real business with genuine scale — the profit problem here is structural, not fundamental; the revenue exists, it just needs to be captured and managed more effectively
The Gap
  • Moving from $100k to $200k in owner compensation — Donna's stated 12-month goal — requires pricing discipline, AR management, and a clear profit plan; more revenue alone will not close this gap if the structural issues remain
  • The $600k–$1M exit target requires EBITDA visibility and a demonstrable profit trajectory — a buyer will want to see at least 12–24 months of clean financial performance, which means the profit plan needs to start now
  • Without practice area profitability data, the firm may be growing its least profitable work — the selective growth strategy Donna described (more estates, less low-value family) requires financial tracking to execute
Every dollar of improvement in collections, pricing discipline, and owner compensation between now and a sale conversation makes that sale worth more — the profit plan is not just about paying Donna what she is worth today, it is about building the asset value she wants to sell.
