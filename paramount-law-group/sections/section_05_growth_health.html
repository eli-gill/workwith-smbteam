Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Referrals and speaking events are the only sources of new clients — no digital marketing system of any kind is running
  • 13 Google reviews vs. Kahane Law at 381 and Osuji & Smith at 467 — invisible to anyone who searches online
  • Paramount does not appear in the local 3-pack for any target practice area: estates, guardianship, family law, or corporate
Every lead that comes in through referral is a lead Donna personally had to earn — there is no system working for her while she sleeps, travels, or takes her Tuesdays off.

Intake

  • Some pre-screening is in place via minimum retainer requirements — but intake is largely manual with no defined follow-up sequence
  • Front desk is underperforming; no consistent intake protocol to handle inquiries quickly and convert them to retained clients
  • No tracking of lead source, conversion rate, or follow-up speed — the firm cannot measure what it cannot see
An intake system that works without Donna is a prerequisite for any vacation longer than a weekend — and a prerequisite for the sale she is targeting.

Self-Managing Team

  • Donna handles HR, staff management, and legal workload simultaneously — losing 8–10 hours per week to people issues alone
  • No KPIs by role, no accountability cadences, no org chart — staff accountability is ad hoc and institutional knowledge lives in people's heads
  • The firm fails the vacation test: Donna cannot step away for two weeks without operations stalling
A Tuesday-through-Thursday schedule and two months in Mexico each year are only possible when the team runs without Donna in the room — that is not where the firm is today.

Profit

  • Firm bills $1.2M+ but collects ~$800k — $150–175k tied in estate and farm matters with no clear collection timeline
  • Owner compensation is ~$100k against $1.2M in billings; no profit plan to systematically close the gap
  • Complex wills and estate files are being underquoted — scope creep is silently eroding margins on the firm's highest-value work
Doubling owner take-home to $200k — the stated goal — requires pricing discipline, AR management, and a profit plan, not just more revenue.
