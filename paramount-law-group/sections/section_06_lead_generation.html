Lead Generation

Paramount Law Group has built a respected practice in Calgary on the strength of Donna Gee's personal reputation and referral network — but there is no marketing system in place to capture the online demand that exists every day for estates, probate, guardianship, and corporate services. Every potential client who searches Google for these practice areas right now is finding a competitor instead.

Website Audit

"Website intentionally underdeveloped to deter recruiter poaching."
The Problem
  • The site was deliberately kept minimal — which means it has no practice area landing pages, no conversion architecture, and no content that search engines can rank for targeted Calgary keywords
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved during research (site blocks automated analysis tools) — Donna should run this manually at pagespeed.web.dev; WordPress sites without optimization typically score 40–65 on mobile
  • No CTA is visible above the fold on the homepage — visitors who arrive have no immediate prompt to call, schedule, or take any action
  • Contact form is on a separate /contact/ page only — not embedded on practice area pages where intent is highest
Blind Spot: The strategy of keeping the website underdeveloped to deter recruiters is costing the firm far more in missed client inquiries than it saves in recruitment friction. Every month, people in Calgary search for "estate lawyer Calgary," "probate lawyer Calgary," and "guardianship application Alberta" and find Kahane Law or McLeod Law — not Paramount. That is not a recruiter problem. That is a revenue problem.
The Positive
  • Individual attorney bio pages exist for at least three lawyers — a foundation that can be strengthened into trust-building content
  • A public testimonials page exists at /testimonials/ — positive social proof that is not yet connected to a review-generation strategy
The Gap
  • Without practice area landing pages and local SEO content, the site cannot rank — meaning every search for the firm's target services goes to a competitor who has invested in these pages
  • A homepage with no above-fold CTA means even the visitors who do find the site are converting at a fraction of what they should — the firm is losing leads it already paid (with Donna's time) to generate
  • Without PageSpeed optimization and mobile performance, Google's algorithm ranks the site lower — compounding the visibility gap
A rebuilt, conversion-optimized website is not a vanity project — it is the foundation that makes every other marketing investment work, and the online face of a firm Donna eventually wants to sell.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Google Business Profile shows 13 reviews at 4.7 stars — a strong rating, but a review count that places Paramount at the bottom of any competitive comparison in the Calgary legal market
  • Paramount does not appear in the local 3-pack for any observed search across its four target practice areas: estates/probate, guardianship/trusteeship, family law, or corporate/commercial
  • Two different addresses appear across directories (401-1110 Centre St NE on Yellow Pages vs. 342 4 Ave SE on ZoomInfo and other databases) — NAP inconsistency actively suppresses local search authority
  • An old "permanently closed" nearby GMB listing is still appearing in local search, confirmed by Donna on the call — this confuses prospects and erodes trust before they ever reach the firm
  • No geo-targeting landing pages exist for surrounding Alberta communities the firm serves — no pages for Airdrie, Cochrane, Red Deer, Lethbridge, or Central Alberta, despite the firm's stated service area
Blind Spot: The Birdeye aggregation also shows a second listing — "Sartore Peter, Law Office (Now with Paramount Law LLP)" — with 17 reviews. This merged listing may be creating a confusing secondary GBP signal that dilutes review authority rather than consolidating it. This should be audited and resolved before any review-generation campaign begins.
The Positive
  • The 4.7-star rating is genuinely strong — if combined with a review-generation campaign, this could become a real competitive asset quickly
The Gap
  • With only 13 reviews vs. Kahane Law's 381 and Osuji & Smith's 467, Paramount simply cannot compete for local 3-pack placement — Google's algorithm weighs review count and recency heavily for local ranking
  • The NAP inconsistency across directories tells Google that the business information is unreliable — this directly suppresses rankings regardless of how good the actual content is
Kahane Law Office — ~381 Google reviews and dedicated, keyword-optimized landing pages for estates and corporate matters. Kahane dominates the local pack for wills, estates, and corporate searches in Calgary because they have invested in both review volume and targeted on-page SEO simultaneously.
Fixing the GBP, resolving the NAP inconsistency, and launching a systematic review-generation campaign are the fastest, cheapest ways to start appearing where clients are already looking — and they directly increase the firm's valuation by improving its online footprint before a sale.
