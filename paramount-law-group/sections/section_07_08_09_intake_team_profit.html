Intake Assessment

Paramount Law Group has basic intake filters in place — minimum retainer requirements and some Cosmolex automation — but intake is largely manual with no defined follow-up sequence, no performance tracking, and a front desk that is acknowledged to be underperforming. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: AMBER
"Front-desk underperformance" and "repeated explanations" — staffing issues at the first point of client contact are costing the firm real revenue every week.
The Problem
  • No structured intake protocol — inquiries are handled differently depending on who answers, with no defined script, qualification criteria, or urgency standard
  • No after-hours coverage — prospects who call evenings or weekends reach voicemail and often do not leave a message, calling a competitor instead before morning
  • No lead tracking or intake reporting — the firm cannot see conversion rates, average time-to-response, or how many inquiries are being lost before they ever reach a lawyer
  • Front desk performance gaps mean that the first impression for many prospective clients is inconsistent — a high-value estate or corporate prospect may hang up before they ever experience Donna's expertise
Blind Spot: Estate planning and guardianship clients are often in emotionally difficult situations — caring for an aging parent, dealing with a recent death, facing a family health crisis. They call looking for reassurance and competence from the first moment. An undertrained or inconsistent front desk is not just a conversion problem — it is a trust problem that can cost the firm its most valuable case types before a consultation is ever scheduled.
The Gap
  • At a 15% close rate and $3,500 average case value, every 10 mishandled inquiries per month costs the firm approximately $5,250 in revenue — improving intake conversion by even 5 percentage points is worth more than most paid ad campaigns
  • Without intake tracking, the firm has no way to know whether lead generation investments are working — every marketing dollar spent on ads or SEO is going into a black box
  • A firm that cannot consistently convert inbound inquiries cannot be sold at a premium — buyers want predictable systems, not owner-dependent processes
Donna's exit goal requires a firm that converts leads predictably without her involvement — that starts with intake, and intake is fixable faster than almost anything else in this audit.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • Contact form exists at /contact/ only — not embedded on practice area pages where visitor intent is highest; a prospect reading about probate services should not have to navigate to a separate page to reach the firm
  • No contact form or CTA is visible above the fold on the homepage — the most common entry point for new visitors offers no immediate conversion pathway
  • No chat or callback option observed — after-hours website visitors have no way to initiate contact until business hours, and those prospects are gone by morning

Team Assessment

Donna Gee is simultaneously the lead lawyer, the managing partner, and the de facto HR director for a ten-person practice — a combination that is consuming 8–10 hours per week in people management alone and making it structurally impossible for her to step away. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED
"HR/people management drains 8–10 hrs/week, causing burnout; staff drama, low accountability, repeated explanations, front-desk underperformance."
The Problem
  • No KPIs by role — staff members have no clear performance standard to hold themselves to, and Donna has no objective basis for feedback or accountability conversations
  • No accountability cadences — no regular structured check-ins, no documented goals, no rhythm by which the team reports progress and escalates issues without going directly to Donna
  • No org structure — institutional knowledge lives in people's heads, not in documented SOPs; when someone leaves, their knowledge leaves with them
  • No documented processes for intake-to-matter workflows — every new hire or transition requires Donna to personally retrain from scratch, a cycle that repeats indefinitely until it is broken by building the SOPs
Blind Spot: The plan to spend two months in Mexico and maintain a Tue–Thu schedule is not a scheduling problem — it is a systems problem. The firm currently fails what we call the vacation test: Donna cannot take two weeks off without operations stalling. Until there is an org structure, documented workflows, role KPIs, and an accountability cadence in place, no schedule change will actually hold.
The Positive
  • Donna already has Tim as a trusted operations partner who is actively engaged and capable of implementing systems — this is a significant head start; most firms at this stage have no one in that role at all
The Gap
  • At $525/hour — Donna's stated time value — the 8–10 hours per week lost to HR management represents a $218,000–$273,000 per year productivity drain, confirmed by Donna on the call
  • Without a self-managing team, a buyer will discount the firm's valuation significantly — a practice that requires the owner to run cannot be priced as a business; it gets priced as a job
  • Every week without KPIs and accountability cadences is a week the team learns that there are no consequences for underperformance — breaking that pattern takes consistent, structured accountability, not a single conversation
The two months in Mexico, the Tue–Thu schedule, and the $600k–$1M exit all require the same thing: a team that performs without Donna in the building — and that is exactly what a Fractional COO engagement is built to create.

Profit Plan Assessment

Paramount Law Group bills over $1.2 million per year but collects approximately $800,000 — and the owner takes home roughly $100,000, which is less than what Donna could earn as a salaried lawyer at another firm. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
"2025 billed ~$1.2–1.25M; collected ~$800k; AR $150–175k (estate/farm tied); debt $100–125k; owner pay ~$100k (wants $200k)."
The Problem
  • $150–175k in accounts receivable is tied in estate and farm matters with no clear collection timeline — this is working capital the firm has already earned but cannot access
  • $100–125k in debt (line of credit and taxes) is creating cash flow pressure that makes it harder to invest in the team improvements and systems the firm needs to grow
  • Complex wills and estate files are being underquoted — scope creep on the firm's highest-value work is silently eroding margins; Donna acknowledged that complex wills are sometimes completed at a loss relative to the time invested
  • Limited KPIs and ad hoc reporting mean there is no clear visibility into which practice areas, case types, or lawyers are most profitable — decisions are made on gut feel, not financial data
Blind Spot: The $400k gap between billings ($1.2M) and collections ($800k) is not just a cash flow issue — it is a valuation issue. A buyer assessing this firm will look at collected revenue, not billed revenue. Every dollar of AR that is chronically uncollected reduces the effective multiple on exit. Cleaning up collections and tightening billing practices is one of the highest-ROI moves available before any sale conversation begins.
The Positive
  • Cosmolex is in place and actively managing trust accounting and retainer top-up automation — a financial management foundation that is ahead of many firms at this stage
  • Revenue at $1.2M+ is a real business with genuine scale — the profit problem here is structural, not fundamental; the revenue exists, it just needs to be captured and managed more effectively
The Gap
  • Moving from $100k to $200k in owner compensation — Donna's stated 12-month goal — requires pricing discipline, AR management, and a clear profit plan; more revenue alone will not close this gap if the structural issues remain
  • The $600k–$1M exit target requires EBITDA visibility and a demonstrable profit trajectory — a buyer will want to see at least 12–24 months of clean financial performance, which means the profit plan needs to start now
  • Without practice area profitability data, the firm may be growing its least profitable work — the selective growth strategy Donna described (more estates, less low-value family) requires financial tracking to execute
Every dollar of improvement in collections, pricing discipline, and owner compensation between now and a sale conversation makes that sale worth more — the profit plan is not just about paying Donna what she is worth today, it is about building the asset value she wants to sell.
