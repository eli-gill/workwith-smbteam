Here Is Where Scully Disability Law Stands Today

Scully Disability Law has served Northwest Indiana and Chicagoland since 1984 — over 40 years helping people win Social Security Disability, SSI, and Veterans benefits on a contingency basis. The firm has built a strong local reputation with two experienced attorneys and four office locations, but its digital presence has not kept pace with the growth of its market, and several key systems that drive consistent, scalable growth are missing or underbuilt.

The Lawyer Legacy Staircase shows the six stages every law firm owner moves through on the path from solo practitioner to true law firm owner — and where your firm stands today.

Stage 3 — Solo Practitioner (Current Stage)
  • You own your firm, but you are the firm
  • If you stop working, revenue stops
  • This is where most law firm owners start — and where too many stay
Stage 4 — Small Business Manager
  • You have at least one system generating leads beyond referrals
  • You have a small team but are still in the middle of everything
  • You are building the foundation for real growth
Stage 5 — Law Firm CEO
  • Multiple lead sources are running and a trained team handles intake
  • Managers are in place and you are working on the business more than in it
  • Real freedom is starting to emerge
Stage 6 — Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible

Overall Growth Stage Assessment

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We help law firm owners get more profit, more personal freedom, and outstanding results for their clients.

SMB Team has worked with hundreds of law firms and built a proven framework for helping law firm owners get three things at once — more profit, more personal freedom, and outstanding results for their clients. The framework is called the Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model. It is built on four pillars that every law firm must master to grow.

  • Lead Generation — A steady source of new clients that does not depend on referrals or the owner's personal network
  • Intake — A system that turns leads into signed clients without the owner handling every call
  • Self-Managing Team — A team that handles casework and daily operations without the owner in every decision
  • Profit Plan — A clear picture of what comes in, what goes out, and what the owner keeps every month

When all four pillars work together, the result is a firm that grows on its own, runs without the owner doing everything, and produces real profit — giving the attorney the income, the freedom, and the impact they set out to create.

A Social Security disability firm's revenue arrives in unpredictable batches tied to case settlements — not a steady monthly paycheck. Without a clear profit plan, overhead from four offices can quietly consume more than the firm realizes. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, the owner cannot know whether their growth is making them wealthier — or just busier.

Profit Plan Assessment
  • SSDI contingency fees are capped by SSA at 25% of back pay (currently up to $9,200 per case) — this means revenue depends entirely on case volume and how long cases have been open
  • Four office locations carry fixed monthly overhead (rent, utilities, staffing) that must be covered regardless of whether cases settle in any given month
  • No financial tracking, margin visibility, or client acquisition cost data is evident from the firm's public presence — without this, it is impossible to know whether any new marketing investment is profitable
A disability firm that wins 100 cases per year at an average fee of $6,000 generates $600,000 in revenue — but if overhead runs $400,000, the owner takes home $200,000. That math only improves with a profit plan that tracks costs and protects margin intentionally.
  • Understanding the cost per acquired client, case win rate, and average settlement fee is the foundation of a profit plan for a disability firm
  • A clear profit plan would allow the firm to invest in marketing with confidence, knowing exactly what return is required to justify each dollar spent
