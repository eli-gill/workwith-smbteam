Lead Generation

Arlington Law Office, PC generates approximately 70% of its new clients from uncontrolled word-of-mouth referrals — a strong signal of client satisfaction but a fragile foundation for a firm trying to reach $1M+. The remaining 30% comes from a small magazine ad budget and discontinued PPC campaigns that were abandoned after two years due to poor conversion results, leaving the firm with no active digital marketing presence in the market right now.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The website (arlingtonlawoffice.com) is live and confirmed active, with structured practice-area subpages and a modern URL architecture — but it blocks external performance testing tools, so PageSpeed scores could not be obtained automatically and must be verified manually at pagespeed.web.dev before the presentation
  • PageSpeed scores: Not obtained — the site's CDN blocks the PageSpeed Insights API. Rep must run manually.
  • No video content observed — attorney trust video is one of the highest-converting elements on a law firm website, and it is missing
  • No confirmed blog or fresh content — without regularly updated content, the site has no mechanism to rank for the long-tail immigration and estate planning searches that drive qualified, research-mode traffic
  • NAP inconsistency detected: the firm's address appears as both Suite 204 and Suite 206 across third-party directories — this signals to Google that the listing data is unreliable and directly suppresses local search rankings
Blind Spot: Competitors like Yekrangi & Associates and Brudner Law have built city-specific landing pages targeting Garden Grove by name. When someone searches "immigration attorney Garden Grove," those pages appear — not the Arlington Law Office homepage. Having a great website is not the same as having a website that ranks for the searches your clients are actually making.
The Positive
  • The site includes a free consultation CTA above the fold and offers remote consultations — removing friction for out-of-area and time-pressured prospects
  • Attorney bio for Rose Bui is present with bilingual credentials, bar admission details, and practice area focus — this builds trust before a consultation is ever scheduled
The Gap
  • Without practice-area-specific landing pages optimized for Garden Grove, Westminster, Anaheim, and surrounding cities, the firm loses every local search to competitors who have already built that infrastructure
  • A website with no video, no blog, and no city-specific pages converts visitors at a much lower rate — the firm is likely losing qualified prospects who visit and then call a competitor whose site builds more trust
  • The NAP inconsistency (Suite 204 vs. Suite 206) is actively hurting Google Business Profile authority and suppressing local map pack visibility across all three practice areas
A website that ranks, converts, and builds trust in your community is not just a marketing asset — it is the infrastructure Rose needs to prove that a solo firm can hit seven figures without relying on whoever happens to refer someone this week.
