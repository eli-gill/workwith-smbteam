Lead Generation

The Law Offices of Justin R. Payne, PA has operated for over two decades on the strength of professional referrals — a solid foundation, but not a scalable system. With no observed digital advertising, an unoptimized Google Business Profile, and an aging website with an active credibility problem, the firm is invisible to the pool of prospective clients who search before they call.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The homepage headline misspells "Commitment" as "Committment" — a credibility-damaging error on the first line a prospective commercial or real estate litigation client reads
  • PageSpeed scores: Not retrieved — manual check required at pagespeed.web.dev; site age and design patterns suggest mobile scores are likely below the 50/100 benchmark
  • No contact form on the homepage — the only conversion path is a phone number, eliminating visitors who arrive outside business hours or prefer not to call first
  • Second domain jrpaynelaw.com is live but not forwarding to cocoaattorney.com — splitting brand authority, losing traffic, and creating a dead end for anyone who finds that URL
Blind Spot: The unforwarded domain and the misspelled headline are not just cosmetic problems — they are trust signals that work against Justin before a prospective client ever reads about his experience. For commercial real estate clients and title companies evaluating whether to refer a litigator, a website that signals inattention to detail can be disqualifying. The firm's referral reputation is stronger than the digital front door currently deserves.
The Positive
  • cocoaattorney.com appears in organic search results for property insurance litigation and civil litigation searches in Cocoa FL — meaningful SEO equity exists from the site's age and practice area content
  • Attorney bio is present with 15+ years of intensive litigation experience noted — a strong credibility element that needs a better-designed frame around it
The Gap
  • Without a contact form above the fold, visitors who arrive outside business hours — when the highest-urgency legal situations often occur — leave without a way to reach the firm
  • A website with a misspelled headline and outdated design signals to commercial clients and title company referral sources that the firm does not invest in its presentation — which raises questions about process and attention to detail
  • Two unconnected domains divide the firm's SEO authority — every link or citation pointing to jrpaynelaw.com is not reinforcing the primary site
A rebuilt, conversion-optimized website is the digital front door to the supervisory-level practice Justin described — prospective clients should find it, trust it, and contact it without Justin having to be in the room.

  • No geo-targeted landing pages for surrounding Brevard County cities (Merritt Island, Titusville, Melbourne, Cape Canaveral) — limiting organic reach to Cocoa alone
Blind Spot: Goldman, Monaghan, Thakkar & Bettin operates from the same building at 96 Willard Street. When a prospective client searches for a real estate or civil litigation attorney in Cocoa and sees two firms at the same address, the multi-attorney firm with the stronger review profile and more established web presence will almost always win the click. Justin's local SEO presence needs to be distinct and authoritative — not a footnote beside a larger neighbor in the same building.

The Gap

  • NAP inconsistencies across directories suppress local ranking and create confusion for prospective clients who find conflicting location information

Their tenure and depth in the exact practice areas Justin handles means they benefit from accumulated directory authority and word-of-mouth momentum that Justin's solo practice cannot match without a deliberate local SEO campaign.
Building a dominant local SEO presence — optimized GBP, consistent directory listings, and an active review generation system — creates the kind of inbound visibility that generates cases without Justin having to personally generate every referral.
