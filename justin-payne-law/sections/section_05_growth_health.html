Pillar Health Scorecard

Lead Generation

  • Referrals are the only consistent source of new clients — no digital system exists
  • No paid advertising observed across Google, LSA, or Meta for any practice area
  • Website has a misspelled homepage headline, no contact form, and an unverified GBP with no recent reviews
A predictable flow of new cases is the foundation everything else rests on — without it, Justin cannot hire, cannot delegate, and cannot build toward the supervisory practice or eventual sale he has described.

Intake

  • Attorney personally handles all intake — no delegation exists for new client inquiries
  • Contact is phone-only with no form on the homepage; firm is unreachable outside M–F 8:30am–5pm
  • No defined intake script, qualification process, or follow-up sequence for unconverted inquiries
Every missed call after hours is a case that went to a competitor — fixing intake converts more of what the marketing produces without spending another dollar on lead generation.

Self-Managing Team

  • Solo practice with one recently hired assistant — owner handles all legal work, admin, and intake
  • 10–20 hours per week spent on non-legal tasks that have not yet been delegated
  • No defined roles, accountability systems, or management structure in place
The wizard practice Justin described — where staff prepares the files and he argues the cases — requires a team structure that does not yet exist; building it is the prerequisite to every other goal.

Profit

  • Annual revenue of ~$350,000 is known, but profit margin, cost per case, and overhead visibility are unclear
  • No financial tracking by practice area — impossible to evaluate the margin potential of a practice pivot without this data
  • No acquisition cost tracking — no basis for measuring whether a marketing investment is producing profitable cases
Knowing exactly what the firm earns, what it keeps, and where margins are strongest is what separates a practice from a business — and a business from a sellable asset.
