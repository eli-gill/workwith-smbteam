Here Is Where PS Law Group Stands Today

PS Law Group is a specialized B2B law firm founded in 2021 by Paula Savchenko in Wilton Manors, Florida, serving regulated industries — cannabis, hemp, kratom, dietary supplements, and tobacco — across eleven states. With revenue growing from $800K to approximately $1 million annually, the firm is at a critical inflection point: Paula herself has become both the firm's greatest asset and its most urgent bottleneck, personally handling every client call, billing decision, case review, and operational task while working 60+ hours per week — with no system in place to convert her expertise into predictable, scalable growth.

Stage 6 is the stage where the firm runs without you — where you can build the AI company you've been imagining, expand into autonomous vehicles, and finally stop being the ceiling on your own growth.

Where You Are Today

Stage 3: Solo Practitioner
  • Every new client comes through word-of-mouth — no paid advertising, no SEO strategy, no predictable inbound system
  • Paula personally handles all client calls, billing, case review, and admin — working 60+ hours per week with no delegation structure
  • No per-case profitability data means hiring and investment decisions are made on gut feel, not financial clarity
  • Monthly revenue ranges from $50K to $100K+ — the inconsistency makes planning a 10-attorney firm nearly impossible

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
The gap between where you are and where you want to be is not about your expertise — it is about the systems you have not built yet, and that is exactly what this plan addresses.

The Law Firm Growth Acceleration Model

We Help Law Firm Owners Get More Profit, More Personal Freedom, And Outstanding Results For Their Clients.

Most law firms try to grow by chasing more leads. But leads alone are not the problem — and leads alone are not the answer. Here is what actually works.

Lead Generation

Leads are to a law firm what gasoline is to a car. Without them, you go nowhere. But a steady, predictable flow of leads changes everything — you get to choose your cases instead of taking whatever comes in.

Intake

But all the leads in the world are worthless without a system to turn them into paying clients. Most firms are losing cases they already paid for simply because their follow-up is slow or inconsistent.

Self-Managing Team

Once clients are coming in, you need a team — otherwise you end up working 90-hour weeks and results suffer. A self-managing team is what gives you the freedom to step back.

Profit Plan

And once the team is in place, most owners look at their bank account and ask: where did all the money go? That is what a profit plan solves — clarity on what you earn, what you keep, and how to grow both.

All four have to work together. If one is missing, you cannot have more profit, more freedom, and outstanding results for your clients at the same time.

Intake Assessment

At PS Law Group, every new client interaction begins and ends with Paula — there is no intake process separate from Paula herself, which means the firm's ability to convert new inquiries is entirely dependent on her availability, energy, and calendar. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Paula personally handles every client call and intake conversation — there is no intake coordinator, no defined screening process, and no delegation in place
  • Small, low-value referral clients ($2K–$4K) are entering the pipeline without any filtering, consuming significant time from both Paula and Jessica despite representing the lowest-margin work in the practice
  • No defined follow-up sequence for inquiries that do not convert immediately — a prospect who reaches out and does not hear back promptly is very likely calling a competitor next
  • No after-hours intake coverage — any inquiry received outside business hours depends entirely on Paula checking her messages, with no systematic way to capture and respond to overnight or weekend B2B inquiries

The Gap
  • Every hour Paula spends on intake — screening calls, initial consultations, follow-ups — is an hour not spent on the higher-value work, business development, or building the AI venture she is simultaneously trying to launch
  • Without a defined intake filter, the firm will continue accepting $2K–$4K clients that take disproportionate bandwidth, preventing the shift to higher-value litigation and federal lobbying work that the practice is already winning
