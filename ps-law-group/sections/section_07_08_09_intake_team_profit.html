Intake Assessment

At PS Law Group, every new client interaction begins and ends with Paula — there is no intake process separate from Paula herself, which means the firm's ability to convert new inquiries is entirely dependent on her availability, energy, and calendar. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Paula personally handles every client call and intake conversation — there is no intake coordinator, no defined screening process, and no delegation in place
  • Small, low-value referral clients ($2K–$4K) are entering the pipeline without any filtering, consuming significant time from both Paula and Jessica despite representing the lowest-margin work in the practice
  • No defined follow-up sequence for inquiries that do not convert immediately — a prospect who reaches out and does not hear back promptly is very likely calling a competitor next
  • No after-hours intake coverage — any inquiry received outside business hours depends entirely on Paula checking her messages, with no systematic way to capture and respond to overnight or weekend B2B inquiries
Blind Spot: B2B clients in regulated industries — cannabis companies, supplement brands, kratom distributors — often make vendor decisions quickly when regulatory pressure hits. A prospective client who contacts PS Law Group on a Friday evening and does not hear back until Monday morning may have already retained a competitor by the time Paula responds. In a niche where urgency is common and compliance windows are narrow, slow intake is a silent revenue killer.
The Gap
  • Every hour Paula spends on intake — screening calls, initial consultations, follow-ups — is an hour not spent on the higher-value work, business development, or building the AI venture she is simultaneously trying to launch
  • Without a defined intake filter, the firm will continue accepting $2K–$4K clients that take disproportionate bandwidth, preventing the shift to higher-value litigation and federal lobbying work that the practice is already winning
  • As PS Law Group starts to receive more inbound leads through digital marketing, an absent intake process will cause a growing percentage of those leads to fall through the cracks before they ever become clients
A defined intake process — with clear criteria, a screener who is not Paula, and a follow-up sequence that runs automatically — is how PS Law Group converts referrals and inbound leads without Paula being in every conversation.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • A contact page is confirmed at pslawgrp.com/contact/ — whether a contact form is present and whether it routes to a monitored inbox could not be verified during research; this should be confirmed and optimized during onboarding
  • No phone number with a clickable hyperlink was observed in the site header — on mobile devices, a non-clickable phone number means interested visitors must manually dial, and many will not bother; adding a hyperlinked number is a 5-minute fix that directly increases inbound calls (this was also an explicit action item on the discovery call)
  • No live chat observed — for B2B prospects conducting research outside of business hours, a contact form or chat option is often the only path to inquiry; without it, any off-hours website visit is effectively a dead end

These findings are also referenced in the Website Audit section.

Team Assessment

PS Law Group has the right people starting to come together — Paula, Jessica, an EA, and a third attorney joining imminently — but the team operates in a structure where every decision, every workflow, and every client deliverable still flows through Paula. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • There are no documented SOPs — without written processes, the team cannot act independently on routine tasks, and every exception requires Paula's involvement
  • No defined roles with clear KPIs — Jessica and the EA are doing work, but without accountability structures there is no way to measure performance or delegate with confidence
  • A third attorney is joining next week, but the firm has no onboarding process — adding headcount without systems in place typically results in more management burden on Paula, not less
  • Paula is responsible for billing and case review across all matters — two of the highest-leverage areas for delegation that would immediately return hours to her week
Blind Spot: Adding attorneys without first defining how work flows, who owns what, and how performance is measured is one of the most common growth traps in law firms. PS Law Group is about to add its third attorney — the exact moment at which informal management becomes unscalable. Building the structure before the next hire, not after, is the difference between scaling and stalling.
The Positive
  • Paula already has a team in place — the raw material for delegation exists; the missing piece is structure, not people
  • The firm is already using Clio for case management — a foundational technology that can support SOPs, workflows, and delegation once it is properly configured
The Gap
  • Every week Paula spends as the operational center of the firm is a week she cannot spend on the client development, new practice area expansion, and AI venture that represent the actual growth trajectory she described
  • Without SOPs and defined roles, each new hire increases complexity rather than capacity — which means Paula's hours don't decrease as the team grows, they increase
  • The 10-attorney firm Paula is building in five years requires a management layer that does not exist today — the time to build it is now, at four people, not at ten
A team with clear roles, documented workflows, and the ability to handle cases from intake to close without Paula in every step is the only path to the firm Paula is trying to build — and to the AI company she wants to build alongside it.

Profit Plan Assessment

PS Law Group has grown revenue from $800K to approximately $1 million without a clear picture of where that revenue is coming from or what it actually costs to generate — making every major financial decision, from hiring to software investments, a calculated guess rather than a data-driven call. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • No per-case or per-practice-area profitability data — the firm cannot identify which work types (cannabis licensing, kratom regulatory, litigation, federal lobbying) generate the highest margin and should be prioritized for growth
  • Hiring decisions are based on workload, not financial metrics — the firm has no way to model the return on adding an attorney versus the cost of that attorney's salary, benefits, and supervision time
  • A $100K AI software investment was committed to without ROI data — a significant capital decision made without the financial infrastructure to assess its impact on firm profitability
  • Federal lobbying work is being underpriced to gain experience, with no defined plan to raise rates once the experience is established — a pricing strategy that costs the firm real money every month it continues
Blind Spot: The $2K–$4K referral clients that are consuming significant time may actually be among the least profitable work in the practice — once attorney time, admin overhead, and billing management are accounted for. Without per-case cost tracking, the firm cannot confirm or disprove this. If these small clients are indeed unprofitable on a fully-loaded basis, eliminating them and redirecting that capacity to litigation and lobbying could be the fastest path to a margin improvement without adding a single new client.
The Positive
  • Revenue is growing — from $800K to approximately $1M — demonstrating real market demand and improving collections discipline
  • The firm has already identified financial visibility as a priority problem on the discovery call — Paula is aware the blind spot exists, which is the first step to closing it
The Gap
  • Without knowing which cases are profitable, every growth investment — whether hiring, marketing, or technology — is made without knowing what it needs to return to justify itself
  • Monthly billings ranging from $50K to $100K+ signal revenue volatility that a CFO-level view could identify, analyze, and address — but without that infrastructure, the volatility continues unchecked
  • The AI software venture Paula is building alongside the firm requires capital planning and cash flow modeling that simply does not exist yet — building the financial foundation now is not optional, it is a prerequisite for the bigger vision
Financial clarity — knowing exactly which cases, clients, and practice areas drive the most profit — is what lets Paula make the confident investment decisions that actually build the firm and fund the AI company she is building alongside it.
