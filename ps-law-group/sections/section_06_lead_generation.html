Lead Generation

PS Law Group has grown to approximately $1 million in annual revenue through word-of-mouth alone — a remarkable achievement that also reveals the single biggest structural risk in the business: there is no system generating the predictable inbound leads that a 10-attorney firm requires, and the B2B clients who need PS Law Group's niche expertise most are currently finding Greenspoon Marder, GrayRobinson, and Mr. Cannabis Law instead.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • Homepage headline reads "Licensing Services — PS Law Group is here to navigate you through the complexities of the licensing process" — functional but generic, with no positioning statement that differentiates Paula from national firms competing in the same space
  • PageSpeed scores were not retrievable during research (site blocks automated access) — performance optimization should be assessed and prioritized during onboarding, as slow load times directly reduce B2B lead conversion
  • Blog is confirmed present but appears dormant — most recent indexed posts are COVID-era content; an inactive blog signals to Google that the site is stale and actively suppresses organic rankings in niche search queries
  • Asheville, NC office is still listed on the site despite being inactive — a confirmed NAP inconsistency that undermines local SEO signals and confuses prospective clients
  • No phone number with a clickable hyperlink in the site header — a basic conversion gap that costs mobile visitors who are ready to call
  • No geo-targeted landing pages, no practice-area-specific content pages for kratom, hemp, supplements, or nicotine — leaving the firm invisible in searches for these sub-specialties
Blind Spot: Because PS Law Group's clients are businesses — not individuals — they often conduct longer, research-driven searches before reaching out. A dormant website that looks like it has not been updated in years sends a signal that the firm may not be keeping up with the rapidly changing regulatory landscape they are supposed to guide clients through. In a niche where credibility is everything, an outdated digital presence is not just a marketing problem — it is a trust problem.
The Positive
  • The site is confirmed live with practice area service pages indexed for cannabis licensing, liquor license, and tobacco license — a foundational structure that can be built upon
  • Paula's About page establishes her credentials and multi-state experience, giving prospective clients a reason to trust the firm once they arrive
The Gap
  • Every B2B cannabis, hemp, or kratom company that searches for legal help and lands on a dormant, unoptimized site is a lost engagement — likely worth $10,000–$200,000 in fees
  • The absence of content and landing pages means the firm cannot rank for any of the dozens of high-intent B2B queries in its practice areas — search traffic that competitors are already capturing
  • A website that does not function as a lead-generation asset forces 100% dependence on referrals, which by definition cannot be predicted or scaled
A rebuilt, high-performing website is the foundation Paula needs to stop being the only source of new business — and start letting the firm's digital presence work for her while she builds the vision she actually came here to build.
