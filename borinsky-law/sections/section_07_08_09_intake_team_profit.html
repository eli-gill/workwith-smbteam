Intake Assessment

Borinsky Law currently has no formal intake process — David handles every incoming inquiry personally, and the remote admin/paralegal role has no documented intake responsibilities or protocols. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
"Lead generation first; admin support in progress with a remote hire."
The Problem
  • David personally handles all incoming inquiries — there is no delegation of intake to the admin/paralegal, meaning David is simultaneously the attorney, the business developer, and the intake coordinator
  • No defined intake protocol exists — no script, no response time standard, no structured discovery question set for new inquiry calls
  • No follow-up system for prospects who inquire but do not immediately sign — a high-stakes gap in a practice where $25,000 cases often require days or weeks of prospect consideration
  • No after-hours intake coverage — calls received outside business hours have no automated response or scheduling mechanism
Blind Spot: In the international tax and UHNW market, prospects are often comparing two or three attorneys before deciding. A firm that responds within hours with a professional, credentialed-sounding intake process wins the engagement — regardless of which attorney is technically more qualified. Without a defined intake protocol before ads launch, David will pay for leads he does not convert.
The Gap
  • Every ad lead that goes unanswered for more than a few hours in a high-stakes practice area is a lead that calls the next firm on the list — at $25,000 average case value, a single missed conversion covers months of ad spend
  • Without a structured follow-up sequence, warm prospects who inquire but hesitate will not hear from Borinsky Law again — and competitors with automated follow-up sequences will close cases David already paid to generate
  • As ad volume increases, David's personal bandwidth to handle every inquiry becomes a hard ceiling on case flow — intake must be delegated before paid lead generation scales
A defined intake process is what turns paid leads into the consistent, predictable income David is specifically trying to create — and it must be built before ads launch, not after the first leads start calling.

Website Contact Forms & Live Chat

  • No website currently exists — there are no contact forms, no scheduling links, and no online intake pathway for prospective clients
  • When the website launches, a prominently placed contact form above the fold on every landing page is essential — this is where paid traffic converts or leaves
  • An online scheduling widget (e.g., Calendly) embedded on landing pages allows high-intent prospects to book a consultation immediately, without waiting for a callback — particularly effective for UHNW and business clients who prefer self-directed scheduling

Team Assessment

Borinsky Law is a one-attorney operation where David personally performs all legal work, business development, and practice management, supported by one early-stage remote admin with no defined role scope. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
"Limited discipline/interest in business ops; prefers practicing over managing."
The Problem
  • David is the entire revenue-generating operation — all client work flows through him personally with no delegation path for billable activities
  • The remote admin/paralegal role is early-stage with no documented responsibilities, no intake scope, and no accountability structure
  • No immediate hiring plan beyond the current admin — the path to delegating client-facing or case management responsibilities is not defined
  • David explicitly described himself as having limited interest in business operations — meaning the firm will not self-organize without deliberate systems and external accountability
Blind Spot: The fact that David prefers practicing over managing is not a character flaw — it is exactly why a structured coaching engagement matters. Without an external accountability framework, the business ops David finds uninteresting will remain undone, and the firm will continue to depend entirely on his personal effort every month.
The Positive
  • David already has a remote admin/paralegal in place — this is a foundation, and with proper role definition, this person can take over intake responsibilities before ads launch
The Gap
  • Every hour David spends on intake calls, scheduling, and administrative tasks is an hour he is not practicing law — the highest-value use of his time — and the financial ceiling on the firm is his personal available hours
  • Without a self-managing team structure, David cannot take even a short absence without the firm stalling — making the personal freedom and financial stability he described impossible to sustain
  • As case volume grows from paid advertising, the absence of a defined team structure means David becomes the bottleneck — intake breaks, case quality suffers, and the income growth he is investing in becomes harder to capture
Building even a minimal self-managing structure — starting with the admin's intake role — is what makes predictable income possible without David personally working harder for every dollar.

Profit Plan Assessment

With revenue of approximately $15,000 per month described as lumpy and inconsistent, and a low six-figure debt from a prior venture creating ongoing financial pressure, David is running a practice without a clear profit plan — meaning growth may produce more work without necessarily producing more stability. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: RED — Critical Priority
"Income volatility; wants to 'stop worrying about money.'"
The Problem
  • Revenue is approximately $180,000 per year but described as lumpy — driven by inconsistent case flow rather than a predictable pipeline, making financial planning impossible
  • Low six-figure debt from a prior venture adds urgency to the revenue situation — David needs consistent cash flow, not occasional large checks, to make meaningful progress on this debt
  • No evidence of a formal profit plan, client acquisition cost tracking, or financial visibility framework — without these, it is impossible to know how much of any new revenue becomes actual take-home income
  • Wide retainer range ($20,000–$40,000) without a defined minimum floor means individual deal terms vary unpredictably, making revenue forecasting unreliable
Blind Spot: Adding paid advertising without a profit plan can actually create a false sense of progress. If David lands more cases but does not have a defined minimum retainer, structured payment collection, or client acquisition cost awareness, he may work more without taking home significantly more — which is the opposite of what he described wanting.
The Positive
  • David's stated average case value of $25,000 and retainer range of $20,000–$40,000 represent premium positioning — he is not underpricing his services, and the unit economics of his practice are strong once case flow becomes consistent
  • His stated revenue goal of $500,000 per year, keeping approximately 80%, reflects clear financial thinking about what he actually wants to earn
The Gap
  • Without a minimum retainer floor, individual case economics vary too widely to build predictable income — David himself noted a preference for consistent business rhythm over lumpy, high-variance revenue
  • The absence of a client acquisition cost framework means David cannot evaluate whether his investment in advertising is producing profitable cases or just more work at a similar margin
  • Without financial visibility, the $500,000 revenue goal and $400,000 take-home target remain aspirational rather than being tracked and managed toward systematically
The predictable income David described — enough to stop worrying about money, pay down his debt, and maintain the lifestyle he wants — only comes when revenue is planned, not discovered at the end of each month.
