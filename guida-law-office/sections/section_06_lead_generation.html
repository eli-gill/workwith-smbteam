Lead Generation

Guida is sitting on one of the most defensible market positions in Massachusetts firearms law — but almost none of that credibility is being converted into a predictable flow of new clients. Website Audit

The Problem
  • The site was inaccessible for automated performance testing during this audit (server returned 403 errors) — PageSpeed scores could not be verified. Manual testing is required before launch; slow mobile load times are one of the most common reasons prospects bounce before ever contacting a firm.
  • No confirmed CTA above the fold — the contact page is a separate URL (/home/contact/), meaning a prospect who arrives on the homepage must navigate to find any way to reach the firm
  • No confirmed live chat or immediate response mechanism — after-hours visitors who cannot reach anyone have no friction-free path to leave a message
  • The blog has only two indexed posts across a three-year span (August 2022 and October 2024) — infrequent content publishing limits SEO authority and gives prospects no reason to return to the site
  • No confirmed geo-targeted landing pages — no dedicated pages for "firearms attorney Woburn MA," "LTC attorney Boston," or other high-intent local searches that drive the most qualified traffic
Blind Spot: The firm's homepage headline positions Jason as "The Expert on Massachusetts Firearms Laws and Regulations" — which is accurate and powerful. But that credibility is not being amplified anywhere on the page with proof: no review count, no case outcomes, no USCCA/US LawShield logos, and no credentials above the fold. Visitors who don't already know Jason have no immediate reason to trust that claim.
The Positive
  • Dedicated practice area pages confirmed for Firearms Licensing, Criminal Law, FBI-NICS Denials, Firearms Rights Restoration, Expert Testimony, and Expungement — giving the site legitimate topical authority across the full practice scope
  • An attorney bio page exists — Jason's background (former Firearms Records Bureau Director, former ADA, USCCA referral attorney) is strong enough to convert skeptical visitors if positioned correctly
The Gap
  • Visitors who cannot find a clear CTA above the fold leave without contacting the firm — every one of those is a potential case that chose a competitor instead
  • Without geo-targeted landing pages, the site cannot rank for local searches like "firearms attorney Woburn" or "LTC denial lawyer Massachusetts" — the highest-converting search types
  • A blog updated once every 18 months does not signal relevance to Google — competitors publishing monthly or weekly content accumulate authority advantage over time that becomes very difficult to reverse
A website that converts at a higher rate means more clients from the same referral volume Jason already has — and the foundation needed to make every paid ad investment work harder. That is how you build a firm worth stepping back from.

Local SEO

  • A NAP inconsistency exists across directories: older listings (Avvo, BusinessYab) show 17 Lark Avenue, Saugus, MA while the current website lists 76 Winn Street, Suite 1A, Woburn, MA — conflicting address data weakens Google Maps ranking signals
  • No dedicated geo-targeted landing pages found for surrounding cities (Woburn, Lynn, Boston, Saugus, Peabody) — these pages are one of the strongest local SEO ranking factors for attorneys
  • Google Business Profile posts and photo activity could not be verified — inactive GBP signals reduce local pack ranking potential
  • Yelp listing not confirmed — a common directory that most competitors maintain and that drives additional citation authority
Blind Spot: The two-address problem is more damaging than it appears. Google uses NAP consistency across directories as a key local ranking signal. Every directory that still shows the Saugus address is actively working against the Woburn GBP listing. This is fixable — but only if every directory listing is updated to match exactly.
The Positive
  • Jason's name appears in organic search results for firearms license attorney Massachusetts and LTC attorney searches — the site has genuine topical authority that is not being fully leveraged
  • Listings confirmed on Avvo (Client's Choice Award, 34 five-star client reviews), Super Lawyers (Rising Star 2014–2019), Martindale, Birdeye, and ReelLawyers — a solid directory foundation exists
The Gap
  • With only ~34 Google reviews versus Peterson's 100+, Jason will lose trust comparisons with any prospect who checks multiple firms before calling
  • The NAP inconsistency suppresses local map pack rankings — even a strong GBP cannot fully rank when Google sees conflicting address data across directories
Law Office of Matthew W. Peterson — publishes regular content targeting Massachusetts LTC law changes (most recent: February 2026), has a dedicated Saugus criminal defense landing page, and holds 100+ Google reviews at 5.0 stars. This is what a firm that has invested in local SEO looks like — and it is the benchmark Jason is being measured against every time a prospect searches.
More reviews and a clean, consistent local presence means more clients find Jason first — and choose him. That is what creates the pipeline that funds the attorney hire that frees him from being the bottleneck.
