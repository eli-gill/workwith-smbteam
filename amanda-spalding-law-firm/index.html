Lead Generation

Intake

Intake Assessment

For a two-person firm that has grown on referrals, intake has worked because the volume has been manageable — but the moment paid advertising turns on and call volume increases, the absence of a structured intake process becomes the most expensive problem in the firm. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Amanda personally handles every intake conversation alongside managing active caseloads — there is no separation between business development and case delivery, which creates a ceiling on both
  • No after-hours coverage exists — calls that come in outside business hours go unanswered, and in a market where prospective clients are searching at night, those calls are going to the next attorney on the list
  • No defined follow-up process for leads who do not sign on the first call — without a structured sequence, warm prospects go cold and are never recovered
  • No call tracking, intake CRM, or lead tracking system — there is no visibility into how many leads are coming in, how many are being lost, or what the close rate actually is
Blind Spot: The current lead volume is low enough that Amanda can manage intake manually without feeling the pain. When LSA activates and call volume triples, that same manual process will lose leads faster than the ads can generate them — and there will be no data to show how many were lost or why.
The Positive
  • Jennifer's 16 years with the firm means she understands the client types, case complexity, and firm standards — she has the institutional knowledge to be trained as a first-touch intake handler without a steep learning curve
The Gap
  • At the firm's stated 15% default close rate, losing even 3 leads per week to missed calls or slow follow-up means 12 lost leads per month — at an average case value of $2,750, that is $33,000/month in uncaptured revenue potential
  • Cold leads from digital advertising convert at lower rates than referrals — without an intake script and objection-handling framework designed for non-referral prospects, ad spend will underperform even in a favorable market
  • Without after-hours answering coverage, a firm running LSA is paying per call for leads that go to voicemail and never convert — a direct waste of ad spend
Every unanswered call and every unworked lead is money that will never make it to the campaign fund or the financial cushion for January — fixing intake is the fastest way to increase revenue without spending more on ads.
