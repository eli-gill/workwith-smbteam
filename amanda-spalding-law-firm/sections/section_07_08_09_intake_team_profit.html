Intake Assessment

For a two-person firm that has grown on referrals, intake has worked because the volume has been manageable — but the moment paid advertising turns on and call volume increases, the absence of a structured intake process becomes the most expensive problem in the firm. Fixing intake means converting more of the leads the firm is already generating — without spending another dollar on marketing.

Status: RED
The Problem
  • Amanda personally handles every intake conversation alongside managing active caseloads — there is no separation between business development and case delivery, which creates a ceiling on both
  • No after-hours coverage exists — calls that come in outside business hours go unanswered, and in a market where prospective clients are searching at night, those calls are going to the next attorney on the list
  • No defined follow-up process for leads who do not sign on the first call — without a structured sequence, warm prospects go cold and are never recovered
  • No call tracking, intake CRM, or lead tracking system — there is no visibility into how many leads are coming in, how many are being lost, or what the close rate actually is
Blind Spot: The current lead volume is low enough that Amanda can manage intake manually without feeling the pain. When LSA activates and call volume triples, that same manual process will lose leads faster than the ads can generate them — and there will be no data to show how many were lost or why.
The Positive
  • Jennifer's 16 years with the firm means she understands the client types, case complexity, and firm standards — she has the institutional knowledge to be trained as a first-touch intake handler without a steep learning curve
The Gap
  • At the firm's stated 15% default close rate, losing even 3 leads per week to missed calls or slow follow-up means 12 lost leads per month — at an average case value of $2,750, that is $33,000/month in uncaptured revenue potential
  • Cold leads from digital advertising convert at lower rates than referrals — without an intake script and objection-handling framework designed for non-referral prospects, ad spend will underperform even in a favorable market
  • Without after-hours answering coverage, a firm running LSA is paying per call for leads that go to voicemail and never convert — a direct waste of ad spend
Every unanswered call and every unworked lead is money that will never make it to the campaign fund or the financial cushion for January — fixing intake is the fastest way to increase revenue without spending more on ads.

Team Assessment

Amanda runs a lean, experienced two-person operation — but with a judicial campaign beginning this year, the same person responsible for every client, every case, and every intake call is also expected to be knocking on doors across Bullitt County. Until this changes, the owner cannot have more free time — no matter how much revenue grows.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Amanda is the only attorney — all client work, all decisions, and all legal judgments run through one person, with no coverage when she is in court, at a campaign event, or unavailable
  • All intake is handled personally by Amanda — there is no division between business development and casework, which limits the firm's capacity to grow even with more leads coming in
  • No formal accountability structure or delegation framework exists — Jennifer's role is well-established by practice, but not by defined process or responsibility
  • Adding more cases directly adds to Amanda's personal workload rather than to the firm's capacity — the current team structure does not scale
Blind Spot: The 6-month judicial campaign timeline will compete directly with firm operations in ways that are easy to underestimate now. When Amanda is at a debate, a fundraiser, or a community event, the firm needs a clear structure to keep running — and right now, that structure does not exist in writing.
The Positive
  • Jennifer's 16-year tenure is an exceptional asset — her institutional knowledge of clients, case types, and firm operations means she can take on expanded responsibilities with minimal ramp-up time
The Gap
  • Without assigning Jennifer a defined first-touch intake role with a script, every new call that comes in while Amanda is away from the office is a lead that goes unworked
  • Without a documented delegation structure, the firm's capacity ceiling during the campaign months will be Amanda's personal availability — not the actual demand in the market
  • Whether the firm closes in December or continues, a clear team structure and defined roles now makes the wind-down or the handoff smoother, faster, and less costly
Structuring Jennifer's role to handle first-touch intake and basic case support is the single team change that gives Amanda the freedom to run a campaign and run a law firm at the same time.

Profit Plan Assessment

Amanda's firm is generating approximately $350,000 per year — a solid baseline for a two-person estate planning and probate practice in a rural Kentucky market. But two specific margin leaks identified on the call are leaving thousands of dollars on the table every month from cases already being completed. Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity. Until there is a clear profit plan, growth may just mean more work without more wealth.

Status: AMBER
The Problem
  • Probate is billed at $250/hour — a rate that Amanda noted may be lower than what the value of the service supports, particularly as the firm pursues a premium brand position in Bullitt County
  • Paralegal time is not billed to clients at all — Jennifer works on every case, but clients are not charged for that time, meaning the firm absorbs a significant cost without recovering it
  • No formal profit plan or financial tracking by practice area — there is no visibility into which case types are most profitable and which are consuming the most time relative to their fees
  • No stated revenue goal for the firm — without a clear target, there is no plan to work backward from and no way to measure whether the 6-month sprint is on track
Blind Spot: With 6 months to maximize take-home income before a potential transition, pricing discipline is worth more now than at any other point in the firm's history. A $250 increase in average probate billing — less than one additional hour billed per case — across 10 cases per month adds $2,500/month to the campaign fund with zero additional client work required.
The Positive
  • Revenue of ~$350,000/year is stable and predictable from the existing referral base, providing a solid floor from which incremental digital revenue is purely additive
  • Amanda has clear visibility into her primary billing structure ($250/hr for probate, ~$3,000 average for estate planning) — more financial awareness than many solo practitioners at this stage
The Gap
  • If Jennifer's paralegal time were billed at even $100/hour for 5 hours per probate case, that adds $500 per case — at 10 cases per month, that is $5,000 per month in revenue from work that is already being done
  • Without a revenue target and a monthly tracking system, there is no early warning if the 6-month sprint falls behind — by the time the shortfall is visible, the window to course-correct has already narrowed
  • Pricing coached by SMB Team's mastermind community — seeing what attorneys in comparable markets charge — gives Amanda the peer validation and data to increase rates with confidence
The goal is to close the firm chapter with maximum cash in the bank — and the fastest way to get there is not just more clients, but more revenue from every client already being served.
