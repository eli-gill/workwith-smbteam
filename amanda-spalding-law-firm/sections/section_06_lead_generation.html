Lead Generation

Amanda Spalding Law Firm has operated for over two decades on the strength of referrals, local newspaper ads, and grocery cart advertising — channels that have sustained the firm but cannot deliver the volume of new estate planning and probate cases needed in the next 6 months. No digital marketing system of any kind is currently active, and no local competitor has yet claimed the paid search channels — making this the most time-sensitive opportunity in the entire audit.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The website appears to be in maintenance or coming-soon mode as of the audit date — a "Site will be available soon" message is surfacing in search results, meaning any prospective client who visits the site encounters a dead end instead of a contact form
  • PageSpeed scores could not be retrieved due to the site's maintenance status — Dan should run pagespeed.web.dev on the live site before the follow-up call to capture these numbers
  • The homepage headline "Your Solution Starts Here" is generic and does not speak to the specific urgency or situation of an estate planning or probate prospect
  • Contact form is buried on a separate page — not visible above the fold — and there is no click-to-call button, no live intake form, and no above-fold conversion path for cold traffic
Blind Spot: A prospective client who searches for an estate planning attorney at 9pm and lands on a maintenance page does not wait until morning — they call the next attorney on the list. In a 6-month revenue sprint, this kind of friction kills growth before it starts.
The Positive
  • The domain amandaspaldinglaw.net has been established for 10+ years — Google has indexed the site and it carries domain authority that a brand-new competitor cannot replicate quickly
  • An attorney bio page and resources page exist, indicating the site has content infrastructure that can be built upon without starting from scratch
The Gap
  • Every cold prospect who finds the site in its current maintenance state cannot take action — no form, no call button, no booking link. In a market where the goal is 10–20 new cases per month, this is an active revenue leak
  • A site without above-fold CTAs, practice area landing pages, or attorney trust signals cannot convert paid traffic even if LSA or Google Ads send leads to it — which means ad spend efficiency suffers until the website issue is resolved
  • Two phone numbers in circulation across directories (502-543-1300 and 502-957-1301) are suppressing Google's confidence in the firm's listings and reducing local search visibility
Getting the website off maintenance mode and adding a clear above-fold call-to-action is a same-week fix that removes a barrier between Amanda and the case volume she needs to fund this campaign.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • The Google Business Profile exists but is explicitly under-optimized — the firm does not appear in the local 3-pack for estate planning or probate keyword searches in the Shepherdsville and Bullitt County market
  • GBP categories, services, photos, posts, and Q&A are incomplete or absent — the profile is not sending Google the signals needed to rank in map results
  • No city-specific or county-specific landing pages exist for the surrounding service area (Nelson, Spencer, Hardin counties) — every competing city search is an invisibility event
  • NAP inconsistency: two different phone numbers appear across directories, which actively suppresses local search rankings
Blind Spot: With approximately 22 Google reviews, Amanda Spalding Law Firm actually has more Google reviews than Kentucky ElderLaw's confirmed count of 9 — but those 22 reviews are invisible to new clients because the GBP profile is incomplete. The social proof exists; the platform to showcase it does not.
The Positive
  • Approximately 22 Google reviews at ~4.1 stars is a competitive foundation — more reviews than at least one of the firm's most prominent local competitors, and a strong starting point for LSA eligibility and map pack ranking
The Gap
  • A fully optimized GBP profile — correct primary and secondary categories, complete services section, regular posts, and active photo uploads — can move a firm into local pack visibility within 30–60 days without any ad spend
  • Eliminating the NAP inconsistency (standardizing the phone number across all directories) is a one-time fix that immediately strengthens local search signals
Kentucky ElderLaw PLLC — has invested in a dedicated Shepherdsville city landing page at shepherdsville.kyelderlaw.com, sending Google a strong local relevance signal that a single-page website or under-optimized GBP cannot match. This is the specific tactic pulling them ahead in local organic results.
A fully optimized GBP profile is the fastest path to local pack visibility without ad spend — and for a 6-month sprint, it is the foundation every other marketing channel is built on.

The Opportunity

  • "Probate attorney Nelson County," "probate attorney Spencer County," and "probate attorney Hardin County" are county-level keyword opportunities that match Amanda's service area and the Zoom-friendly probate counties she wants to prioritize

Blind Spot: Because no local competitor is currently running paid search ads in this market, the first firm to activate will set their own cost-per-click floor. Once a competitor enters the auction, those costs increase and the early mover keeps their historical cost advantage. This window is open right now — it will not stay open indefinitely.
