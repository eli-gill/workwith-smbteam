Lead Generation

Adler Law has made a serious marketing investment — Scorpion is delivering 100+ internet leads per month and the firm's geo-targeting infrastructure spans Nassau County and beyond. But at $10,000 per month with a consultation-to-client conversion rate that has dropped 30 points, the firm is losing significant revenue not from a lack of leads, but from a system that is not keeping pace with the volume it is generating.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • The site is built and managed entirely by Scorpion — giving the firm limited control over its own digital presence; the vendor contract means the website, the ad account, and the campaign data belong to Scorpion, not to Adler Law
  • PageSpeed scores: Mobile and desktop scores could not be verified — the site returns access errors to automated tools. Must be run live at pagespeed.web.dev before this presentation. Scorpion sites vary widely; slow mobile performance is a common issue on the platform
  • Yelp lists the primary business category as "Real Estate Law" rather than "Estate Planning" — an inconsistency across directory listings that may reduce Google's confidence in the firm's primary practice and depress estate planning search rankings
  • A FindLaw duplicate listing appears under a Garden City address alongside the primary Jericho listing — creating conflicting signals about the firm's primary location that can hurt local ranking authority
Blind Spot: Competitors may be bidding on branded terms like "Adler Law" or "Steven Adler attorney" as Google Ads keywords — sending name-search traffic to their own sites before prospects ever reach sawlaw.com. This type of conquest bidding is common in competitive legal markets and goes undetected without monitoring.
The Positive
  • Geo-targeting infrastructure is extensive — dedicated city pages confirmed for Jericho, Massapequa, Dix Hills, Garden City, Levittown, Nassau County, and over a dozen additional locations, each with sub-pages by practice area; this is a real competitive asset
  • Attorney bios for Steven Adler, Marissa Caputo, and Andrew Stamas are present and indexed — giving the site the credibility signals that prospects look for when comparing firms
The Gap
  • If PageSpeed mobile scores are below 50, the site is losing visitors before they read a word — Google also penalizes slow mobile sites in organic rankings, eroding the SEO value of all those geo-targeting pages
  • The Scorpion contract locks design, content, technology choices, and campaign data — any transition to a different platform or vendor means starting over with no access to years of accumulated performance history
  • Category inconsistencies on Yelp and FindLaw reduce Google's confidence in the firm's primary practice area, making it harder to rank for the highest-revenue estate planning and probate search terms
A website that the firm actually owns — with independent analytics, proper category signals, and no vendor lock-in — is the foundation Marissa needs to run a firm that grows on its own terms.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • Adler Law has 82 Google reviews at 4.9 stars — but Davidov Law Group has 250+ five-star Google reviews, a 3-to-1 advantage that makes Davidov appear as the trusted default for Nassau County estate planning families doing comparison searches
  • A third-party Local SEO audit (Stormrage) logged a local visibility score of 54% for Adler Law — indicating citation gaps and directory inconsistencies that are holding down local search rankings across the board
  • Local 3-pack positions for primary search terms — "estate planning attorney Jericho NY," "probate attorney Nassau County NY," and related queries — could not be confirmed as dominant; must be verified live before this presentation
Blind Spot: The FindLaw directory shows two separate profiles for the firm — one in Jericho and one showing a Garden City address. Duplicate or conflicting listings confuse Google about which location is primary and split the domain authority that should be concentrated on the main Jericho office, depressing rankings across all targeted locations.
The Positive
  • 82 Google reviews at 4.9 stars represents genuine credibility that took years to earn — many competitors in this market have fewer, and this is the foundation on which a dominant review profile can be built
  • Geo-targeting pages for 10+ Nassau County and Long Island cities extend the firm's SEO footprint well beyond Jericho, giving the site ranking opportunities across the full service area
The Gap
  • Every month Davidov Law Group continues adding reviews and widening their trust advantage — without a systematic post-matter review request process, this gap becomes permanent and increasingly difficult to close
  • A 54% local visibility score means roughly half the directory placements that should be sending leads to Adler Law are either missing, incorrect, or inconsistently listed — a correctable problem that is actively costing the firm in organic rankings
Davidov Law Group — with 250+ five-star Google reviews, Davidov dominates the trust signal for Nassau County estate planning. Their review velocity means the gap is widening every month, not narrowing. Without a systematic review-building program, Adler Law cannot close this gap.
The reviews and geo-targeting pages Adler Law already has are a real competitive asset — building systematically from 82 reviews toward 200+ is not a fantasy, it is a six-month project that positions the firm for Marissa to inherit a dominant online presence.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • Adler Law invests $10,000/month in Scorpion marketing, which typically includes Google Ads management — however, ads for the firm did not appear for the specific keyword searches conducted across all five practice areas during this audit. This may reflect geo-targeting parameters, ad scheduling, or budget concentration on select terms set by Scorpion
  • The firm may be getting outbid by competitors on key estate planning and probate search terms, or ad visibility may be limited to a specific geographic radius that excluded some searches — this requires a direct review of Scorpion's campaign performance dashboard to assess
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Fratello Law (Syosset, NY) — appeared prominently in estate planning searches for the Jericho area; has dedicated geo-targeting landing pages and appears to invest in both organic SEO and paid visibility for Nassau County estate planning keywords
  • Probate Attorney Nassau (probateattorneynassau.com) — a dedicated single-practice site aggressively targeting "probate attorney Nassau County" searches; a highly focused competitor capturing high-intent probate prospects before Adler Law's broader practice site appears
  • Joan Agostino PC — visible for Nassau County real estate attorney searches; a specific paid search threat to the firm's real estate practice area revenue
The Opportunity
  • "Estate planning attorney [city]" searches aligned with the firm's existing geo-targeting pages — Jericho, Hicksville, Hempstead, Garden City, Levittown, and a dozen more cities already have dedicated landing pages; pairing paid ads with those pages is a direct multiplier on existing infrastructure
  • "Probate attorney Nassau County NY" and "probate lawyer Long Island NY" — high-intent searches where the firm has page infrastructure but ad presence was not confirmed for these specific terms
  • Competitor name bidding on "Davidov Law" and "Fratello Law" — capturing prospects who are already searching for named competitors and presenting Adler Law as the credible alternative when they are actively comparing firms
  • "What happens if you die without a will" and "how to avoid probate New York" — educational search intent keywords that capture prospects earlier in the decision cycle at lower cost per lead than high-competition transactional terms
Blind Spot: The current Scorpion arrangement includes ad management, but the firm does not own the ad account. When the relationship with Scorpion ends, the campaign history, quality scores, keyword performance data, and conversion tracking all go with them — meaning any new vendor starts from scratch with no accumulated intelligence.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence
  • Adler Law does not appear to be active in Local Service Ads for primary estate planning and probate searches — this represents a significant visibility gap at the very top of Google results, above all other placements
  • With 82 verified Google reviews and a 4.9-star rating, the firm is well-positioned for the Google Screened background check and verification process required for LSA enrollment
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Specific LSA appearances by named local competitors could not be confirmed during this research — must be verified live by checking the top of Google results for "estate planning attorney Nassau County NY" and "probate attorney Long Island NY" before this presentation
  • Estate planning is a lower-competition LSA category compared to personal injury or criminal defense — meaning the cost per lead and barrier to entry are more favorable for Adler Law to capture top placement
The Opportunity
  • At $6,000 average case value, even an LSA cost per lead of $150–$300 produces a strong return — and with estate planning being a lower-competition LSA category, those costs are achievable in Nassau County
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • The Google Screened badge that appears on LSA listings is a trust signal that matters to the estate planning demographic — families choosing an attorney to handle wills, trusts, and probate decisions respond to verified credentials displayed prominently on the search results page
Blind Spot: Google's LSA Terms of Service update in April 2025 changed participation requirements for attorneys. This must be reviewed with an LSA specialist before enrollment to ensure full compliance with applicable professional responsibility rules in New York.
