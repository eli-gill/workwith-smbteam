Lead Generation

RHD Legal has built a real lead generation engine in under two years — paid search, local SEO, a growing review strategy across four Google Business Profiles, and a referral network that currently delivers 60% of signed cases. The critical gap is that every one of those systems depends on Richard personally managing them, and the one channel where North Carolina PI competitors are most vulnerable right now — Facebook and Instagram video advertising — is currently untapped.

Website Audit

The Problem
  • No video presence on the homepage or practice area pages — in a market where Richard identified video as his single most important growth lever for 2026, the website does not support that strategy with any attorney-led content
  • PageSpeed scores: Pending manual verification — run pagespeed.web.dev before presenting this report (API rate-limited during research; site renders dynamically)
  • No live chat — visitors arriving after hours or who prefer instant engagement over form submission have no immediate option, and in PI, where the decision window is short, every unanswered visit is a potential case lost to a competitor
  • The firm's brand differentiator — Richard's customer service philosophy, his personal commitment to client access, the story behind why he left to start RHD Legal — is not prominent enough above the fold to convert a skeptical PI prospect who is comparing three firms simultaneously
Blind Spot: Competitors may be bidding on "RHD Legal" as a branded keyword in Google Ads — capturing prospects who already know the firm's name and are searching for the website directly. This is a common and inexpensive tactic that large NC PI firms use against growing competitors, and it is invisible until you search your own firm's name.
The Positive
  • Contact form is embedded above the fold on the homepage — one of the most conversion-critical design decisions in PI web design, and one that many competitor sites do not get right
  • Blog is actively updated (most recent post: April 1, 2026) and the firm is already ranking in AI-generated search results for pharmaceutical errors — a niche almost no NC competitor has invested in
The Gap
  • Without video on the site, the website cannot do what Richard described — build the brand recognition that makes prospects feel like they already know him before they ever call, lowering the barrier to signing
  • A website that lacks conversion optimization for paid traffic — no dedicated landing pages matched to specific ad groups — means PPC and LSA traffic arriving at the homepage converts at a meaningfully lower rate than it should
  • Every hour Richard spends reviewing blog drafts, approving web updates, and managing the SEO team is an hour not spent on the $10,000-per-hour work of growing the firm — and a properly built system would make most of that unnecessary
A website that builds trust, generates consultations, and runs without Richard approving every update is one less thing standing between him and the CEO role he described.

Local SEO

The Problem
  • RHD Legal has four Google Business Profiles with a combined 100+ reviews and a 5.0 rating — but each individual profile has significantly fewer reviews than the dominant Raleigh competitors: Whitley Law Firm (589 reviews), James Scott Farrin (504 reviews), and Hardison & Cochran (239 reviews). Each profile competes independently, meaning none reaches the thresholds that trigger LSA dominance on its own.
  • RHD Legal does not appear in the local 3-pack for high-volume Raleigh keywords such as "personal injury lawyer Raleigh NC" — confirmed directly during the discovery call via screen share. Richard has made a strategic decision to deprioritize central Raleigh in favor of less saturated sub-markets, which is smart, but it also means the largest single market in the service area is not generating organic inbound volume.
  • Large firms like Morgan & Morgan and James Scott Farrin are systematically consolidating review counts from multiple locations into single profiles to dominate LSA rankings — a strategy that is directly suppressing RHD Legal's LSA lead volume, which has dropped from approximately 30 leads per month to a fraction of that figure.
Blind Spot: Wake Forest is 20–25 reviews away from the threshold where a concentrated push could lock down the top three map pack positions within the 5-mile radius. At 26 reviews and bouncing between positions 1–3 today, a single focused sprint to 50 reviews could convert an unstable ranking into a defensible local market hold before James Scott Farrin's Wake Forest presence grows strong enough to push RHD Legal out.
The Positive
  • In Wake Forest, RHD Legal is ranking in positions 1–3 in the local pack within a 5-mile radius — Richard confirmed this on the call, and it validates the strategy of targeting less saturated suburban markets ahead of the big firms
  • Geo-targeting landing pages exist for every major market the firm serves, Spanish-language pages are being added, and the firm is ranking in AI-generated search results for pharmaceutical errors — a niche almost no NC competitor has claimed
The Gap
  • Wake Forest needs 20–25 more reviews before end of Q2 to solidify map pack position — the window to do this before James Scott Farrin's Wake Forest presence grows is narrow
  • Southern Pines has 7 reviews on a two-week-old profile — early and coordinated review investment here captures a fast-growing market (the PGA is building a major facility nearby) before larger firms plant a flag
James Scott Farrin — Has a dedicated Wake Forest office, 387+ reviews in that market, and statewide advertising that keeps their name in front of every NC driver who has been in an accident. Their presence in Wake Forest is growing, and every month without a review acceleration strategy in that sub-market narrows RHD Legal's window to hold its current ranking.
Owning the map pack in Wake Forest, Southern Pines, and each new market RHD Legal enters means opening an office and immediately having inbound lead flow — without Richard personally running a marketing campaign for each location.

Google Ads

Ad Presence
  • RHD Legal's Google PPC campaigns were confirmed as actively running but temporarily paused for optimization at the time of this research. Richard confirmed the campaigns were running at $700/day, previously drove signed case volume from 30/month to approximately 40/month, and a Spanish-language PPC campaign had launched 2–3 days prior. Ads may not currently appear in all searches while optimization is in progress.
  • At approximately $500 per click for Raleigh PI terms — confirmed on the call — even a modest improvement in landing page conversion rate or ad relevance score can meaningfully reduce cost-per-case and increase total case volume from the same budget
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • James Scott Farrin — Dominant statewide PPC presence built over decades; their campaigns appear for virtually every PI keyword in NC with high-budget ad groups, optimized Quality Scores, and landing pages refined over thousands of conversion data points
  • Hardison & Cochran — Confirmed multi-platform advertising including Google, running consistent campaigns across NC with brand recognition and review volume that supports higher Quality Scores and lower cost-per-click than newer competitors can achieve
The Opportunity
  • Personal injury and car accident keywords in Wake Forest and Southern Pines are significantly less expensive than Raleigh — extending campaigns into these sub-markets lowers cost-per-click while targeting areas where RHD Legal already has a physical office and map pack presence to support conversion
  • Pharmaceutical errors PPC is almost completely uncontested in NC — RHD Legal ranks organically for this niche and could dominate paid results for pharmaceutical error and medication error terms with minimal spend relative to the case value these matters generate
  • Spanish-language PPC targeting NC's growing Hispanic population is barely utilized by competitors — the campaign just launched and represents a meaningful untapped audience that Richard's newly hired Spanish-speaking paralegal is positioned to convert
  • Retargeting campaigns following website visitors across the Google Display Network are not currently running — a significant conversion gap given that PI case decisions are heavily researched over multiple days, and every visitor who leaves without signing is a prospect that a well-run retargeting campaign can bring back
Blind Spot: At $500 per click in Raleigh, sending paid traffic to the homepage instead of a purpose-built landing page matched to the specific ad is one of the most costly and common mistakes in PI paid search. A landing page specifically built for "car accident attorney Wake Forest" — with a single call-to-action, no navigation, and Richard's face and story above the fold — can double conversion rate from the same click budget.

Local Service Ads

LSA Presence

  • The firm is Google Screened (required for law firm LSA participation) and was previously generating approximately 30 qualified leads per month from LSA before review volume competition intensified — the infrastructure is in place; the review counts are the limiting factor
Competitor LSA Landscape
  • Morgan & Morgan — Just entered NC with a systematic strategy of importing reviews from hundreds of offices nationwide to rapidly build single-profile review counts; already suppressing RHD Legal's LSA visibility in the markets where both firms appear
  • James Scott Farrin — Dominant LSA presence across NC built on years of review accumulation and a 14-office statewide network; their single-profile review counts are multiples of any individual RHD Legal profile
The Opportunity
  • A coordinated review generation push across all four GBP profiles — targeting 50 reviews at Wake Forest and active growth at both Raleigh profiles — directly increases LSA placement frequency and reduces the per-lead cost within 60–90 days
  • LSAs appear above everything else on Google — above paid ads, above the map pack, above all organic results — and you only pay when a real prospect calls you directly.
  • Richard's existing follow-up sequence is already generating reviews efficiently — systematizing and accelerating this process as a team-owned workflow (not Richard's personal task) compounds into more LSA leads every month
Blind Spot: The $50,000 monthly budget cap sitting largely unspent is not a budget problem — it is a review count problem. Every dollar invested in a coordinated review outreach process returns more in LSA lead volume than the same dollar spent on ads at current visibility levels. The fastest path back to 30 LSA leads per month runs through review count, not ad spend.

Facebook & Instagram Ads

Ad Presence
  • RHD Legal does not currently have active Facebook or Instagram ad campaigns — Richard confirmed on the call that he has been manually producing AI-generated video content after hours but has no systematic social advertising program in place. This is the primary reason for this engagement.
  • Richard's own research confirmed that local NC PI competitors are not running Meta retargeting campaigns — he tracks this by visiting competitor pages and checking whether he gets served their ads. This creates a genuine first-mover window: whoever starts advertising first builds the retargeting audience before anyone else can.
Competitor Ad Landscape
  • Hardison & Cochran — The most digitally active competitor in the Raleigh PI market; confirmed running active advertising across Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and LinkedIn, along with TV campaigns in three NC markets. They are building brand recognition across every channel simultaneously while RHD Legal has zero Meta presence.
  • Whitley Law Firm — Active Facebook presence with consistent organic posting and an established audience; specific paid ad activity not confirmed but their organic social reach gives them retargeting audiences that RHD Legal currently cannot build
The Opportunity
  • Retargeting — website visitors who did not convert are leaving with no follow-up and no reason to return
  • Video ads featuring the attorney — builds trust before a prospect ever calls, which is exactly the brand-awareness play Richard described as his primary growth strategy for 2026
  • Educational content ads — capture leads earlier in the decision process before they start searching Google at all
  • AI avatar video campaigns deployed at scale across Meta — this is precisely what Richard has been building manually at midnight; a systematic campaign that produces and distributes that content without his personal involvement every night puts the brand-awareness engine on autopilot and eliminates the midnight work sessions
