Here Is Where RHD Legal Stands Today

RHD Legal is a personal injury firm founded in July 2024 by Richard Dingus, operating out of four offices across North Carolina — two in Raleigh, one in Wake Forest, and a newly opened location in Southern Pines — with car accidents as the primary practice area and a growing niche in pharmaceutical errors that competitors have largely ignored. The firm crossed $600,000 in revenue in its first year and is on track to surpass $1 million this year, signing approximately 30 cases per month across a team of six. The single most urgent finding: Richard is personally running marketing, managing paid ad campaigns, creating video content after hours, handling casework, and building out new markets simultaneously — and without systems that operate without him, the multi-state firm he has mapped out will always be limited by the hours he can personally put in.

Stage 6 is the stage where RHD Legal runs without Richard in the day-to-day — where he works on five to ten cases by choice, shows up to court twice a year because he wants to, and spends the rest of his time on community work, strategy, and the multi-state expansion he has already mapped out on his three-year vision board.

Where You Are Today

Stage 4: Small Business Manager
  • Running PPC, LSA, SEO, and referral networks simultaneously — but managing every one of them personally alongside casework and office expansion
  • Signing 30 cases per month with 60% coming from referrals and 40% from paid marketing — strong results, but dependent on Richard's personal involvement at every step
  • Working until midnight creating AI video content the night before this call — wearing the marketing director, attorney, and CEO hat all at once
  • Team of six experienced, loyal people who execute cases well but have no one empowered to run marketing or business development without Richard's direct involvement

Where You Need To Be

Stage 6: Law Firm Owner
  • The firm runs without you
  • Your income is no longer tied to your personal hours
  • Your time is yours to spend however you choose
  • This is where true freedom and true wealth become possible
Richard built more in 18 months than most attorneys build in a decade — the gap between where he is and where he wants to be is not impossible to close, but it will not close on its own.
Overall Growth Stage Assessment
RHD Legal
RHD Legal is operating at Stage 4 — a Small Business Manager who has multiple lead generation systems in place and a strong intake operation, but who is personally running all of them while simultaneously handling casework, building new office locations, and creating marketing content at midnight. The most urgent limiting factor is not leads or revenue — it is leverage: Richard is doing $10-per-hour work at $10,000-per-hour capacity, and every hour spent on tasks that a well-built system could handle is an hour the firm cannot grow beyond him. Until Richard has the marketing infrastructure, delegation framework, and operating cadence that let the firm run without his daily involvement, the multi-state, multi-attorney practice he described in his three-year vision will remain exactly one person away from stalling.
