Build a Review Generation System
Lead Gen Long-Term Play
Why It Matters
The firm has approximately 2 total reviews across all platforms. Norte Immigration has 228. Musani Law has 128. Review count is the primary factor in Google local pack rankings — and the firm cannot appear in the top 3 with the current review gap regardless of how well the website is optimized.
Who Is Doing It Better
Musani Law — 128 Google reviews at 5.0 stars; their review velocity suggests a systematic post-case review request process that runs automatically after each matter closes.
The Opportunity
A simple post-case review request workflow — text or email sent to every closed client with a direct link to the Google review page — can build 20–30 reviews within the first 90 days from existing and past clients, immediately improving local search visibility at zero ad spend.
Set Up Revenue Tracking by Case Type
Profit Quick Win
Why It Matters
The firm does not currently track revenue by practice area — meaning Eon cannot answer whether immigration or federal criminal generates more profit per hour. Without that data, every marketing decision and every growth investment is a guess.
The Opportunity
Setting up a simple spreadsheet or practice management tracking system that logs each new matter by type, fee, and close date takes less than a day to implement and immediately creates the financial baseline that every future hiring, marketing, and scaling decision will depend on.
Define After-Hours Response Protocol
Intake Quick Win
Why It Matters
Immigration detentions and criminal arrests do not happen on a business-hours schedule. A prospect calling at 9pm who reaches voicemail will call the next firm on the list by morning — and at $5,100+ average case value, each missed after-hours call is a significant revenue loss.
The Opportunity
A defined after-hours protocol — whether a professional answering service, a clear voicemail promise with a guaranteed morning callback, or a text-based intake form — ensures no high-urgency prospect falls through the cracks outside of business hours.
